# Recurrent Miscarriage & Subsequent Pregnancy Outcomes

A curated collection of academic papers investigating whether women with a history of
recurrent miscarriage (RM) experience higher rates of placental complications in
subsequent pregnancies that progress to viability.

## Research Question

**Do women with ≥2 or ≥3 prior pregnancy losses face elevated risk of placental
dysfunction disorders (preeclampsia, placental abruption, gestational hypertension,
preterm birth, SGA, stillbirth) in later viable pregnancies?**

The underlying hypothesis: recurrent miscarriage and placental complications lie on the
same pathological continuum, sharing mechanisms such as defective trophoblast invasion,
thrombophilia, and immunological factors.
