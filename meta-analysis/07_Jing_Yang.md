# Yang, 2017 — Meta-Analysis Extraction

## Group A: Study Characteristics
- **Authors**: Jing Yang, Yan Wang, Xiao-ye Wang, Yan-yu Zhao, Jing Wang, Yang-yu Zhao
- **Year**: 2017 (received April 17, 2017; revised June 15, 2017; accepted June 18, 2017; published July 17, 2017)
- **Journal**: BioMed Research International (Hindawi; 2017, article ID 4359424, 7 pages; DOI: https://doi.org/10.1155/2017/4359424)
- **Country**: China (Beijing)
- **Data source**: Computerized perinatal database of Peking University Third Hospital; obstetric and perinatal information recorded directly after delivery by three obstetricians; data examined routinely by two named co-authors before database entry; coding from medical prenatal care records and routine hospital documents
- **Study design**: Retrospective cohort study
- **Study period**: January 2010 to September 2013

## Group B: Population
- **Total N**: 492 singleton pregnant women (164 FRSA study group + 328 controls); derived from 12,811 total singleton patients who delivered at Peking University Third Hospital during the study period
- **N RM group**: 164 (1.28% of total deliveries meeting inclusion criteria)
- **N control group**: 328 (randomly selected at 1:2 ratio from 12,647 cases without FRSA history delivering in the same period)
- **Inclusion criteria (FRSA group)**: Women who had ≥2 abortions during early pregnancy (first trimester); chromosome karyotypes of both partners normal; no abnormal reproductive tract anatomy; normal thyroid and endocrine functions and insulin level; TORCH IgM negative; antiphospholipid and antinuclear antibodies negative; normal coagulation function; negative cervical secretion; negative for hepatitis B, hepatitis C, syphilis, AIDS, or other infectious diseases; blocking antibodies not detected among patients with normal results in above examinations. Only unexplained FRSA included.
- **Inclusion criteria (control group)**: Singleton pregnant women delivering at Peking University Third Hospital in the same period without history of FRSA; randomly selected at 1:2 ratio.
- **Exclusion criteria**: Medical complications such as chronic hypertension, chronic kidney diseases, autoimmune diseases, and severe late-term pregnancy complications transferred from other hospitals. Women with known causes of abortion (any of: abnormal karyotype, reproductive tract anomaly, thyroid/endocrine abnormality, positive TORCH IgM, antiphospholipid/antinuclear antibodies, abnormal coagulation, positive cervical secretion, infectious diseases, detectable blocking antibodies) excluded from FRSA group.
- **Maternal age RM group**: 33.97 ± 4.25 years (mean ± SD)
- **Maternal age control group**: 30.48 ± 3.65 years (mean ± SD); p < 0.001
- **BMI RM group**: 23.8 ± 3.3 kg/m²
- **BMI control group**: 24.2 ± 2.9 kg/m²; p > 0.05 (not significantly different)
- **Parity**: RM group: primiparous 145/164 (88.4%); control group: primiparous 283/328 (86.2%); p > 0.05 (not significantly different)
- **ART/IVF rate**: RM group 17/164 (10.3%); control group 26/328 (7.9%); p > 0.05 (not significantly different)
- **Smoking**: NR

## Group C: Exposure & Control Definition
- **RM threshold**: ≥2 abortions during early pregnancy (first-trimester recurrent spontaneous abortion, FRSA)
- **Type of losses included**: Unexplained first-trimester losses only (within 12 weeks); women with known causes of abortion excluded from FRSA group; blocking antibodies also assessed
- **Gestational age cutoff for "miscarriage"**: First trimester (within 12 weeks of pregnancy); paper states FRSA "occurs within 12 weeks of pregnancy." Chinese definition of RSA: consecutive fetal loss (body weight <1000 g) before 28 gestational weeks more than 2 times with same sex partner — but FRSA is specifically first-trimester (within 12 weeks).
- **Dose-response analysis**: No — only ≥2 FRSA vs. no FRSA; number of miscarriages within group not analyzed separately
- **Control group definition**: Randomly selected at 1:2 ratio from women delivering at the same hospital during the same period without any history of FRSA; note: hospital is a university-affiliated high-class hospital and regional intensive care referral center, so controls may also represent higher-risk pregnancies

## Group D: Outcomes & Methods
- **All outcomes measured**: Maternal outcomes: cesarean section; postpartum hemorrhage. Placenta-derived maternal complications (PMPC — group of maternal complications caused by insufficient placental implantation and placental ischemia): early-onset preeclampsia (PE; onset <32 weeks); late-onset PE (onset ≥32 weeks); oligohydramnios (excluding cases caused by fetal malformations and maternal PROM); early-onset FGR (first diagnosed <32 weeks); late-onset FGR (first diagnosed ≥32 weeks); placental abruption; late abortion (second-trimester abortion); preterm birth (<37 weeks); very preterm birth (<32 weeks). Placental diseases: placenta previa; placenta accreta; placenta increta and placenta percreta. Perinatal outcomes: small for gestational age (SGA); perinatal death; neonatal severe asphyxia; birth defects of newborns. Gestational age at delivery; birth weight.
- **Effect measure**: OR (odds ratio) for individual outcomes; adjusted OR from multivariate logistic regression for selected outcomes
- **Statistical method**: Independent sample t-test (continuous variables); chi-square test (categorical variables); multivariate logistic regression analysis with backward elimination for association between FRSA and adverse outcomes; SPSS version 19.0
- **Confounders adjusted**: Multivariate logistic regression (Table 6) adjusts for maternal age (and implicitly other factors through the backward elimination approach) for: PE (combined), oligohydramnios, late abortion, and cesarean section. Late-onset PE additionally adjusted for maternal age separately (Table 4). No comprehensive multivariable adjustment for all outcomes — crude ORs presented in Tables 2, 3, 5; adjusted ORs in Tables 4 and 6.

## Group F: Quality Notes
- **Data source quality**: Single tertiary university hospital (regional referral center); computerized perinatal database with quality controls (two named authors reviewed data before entry). Limitation: Peking University Third Hospital is a university-affiliated high-class hospital and regional intensive care referral center, so controls may also represent higher-risk pregnancies, potentially attenuating true differences. Sample size relatively small (164 vs. 328). No intervention was administered during pregnancies (allowing natural history observation).
- **Confounder adjustment**: Partial — multivariate logistic regression for selected outcomes (PE, oligohydramnios, late abortion, cesarean section). Age adjusted for late-onset PE. Crude ORs for most outcomes. Significant confounders: maternal age (33.97 vs. 30.48 years, p < 0.001); gravidity (3.9 vs. 2.2, p < 0.001). BMI and parity were not significantly different. ART not significantly different.
- **Outcome validation**: Physician-coded from medical records and perinatal database; clinical diagnosis using specified cutoffs (e.g., 32 weeks for early/late FGR and PE classification). Pre-eclampsia, FGR, oligohydramnios definitions based on clinical criteria; no ICD coding mentioned.
- **Bias acknowledged by authors**: Yes — (1) sample selection bias in retrospective study; (2) incomplete exclusion of abortion causes (false positive FRSA diagnosis possible); (3) control group also high-risk (referral hospital), weakening differences; (4) small numbers for some outcomes (early-onset FGR, placenta increta/percreta) limit conclusions; (5) FVL and coagulation factor testing recommended but not routinely performed in this Chinese Han population. Authors note multicenter study needed.
- **Other notes**: This study is unique in subdividing PE into early-onset (<32 weeks) and late-onset (≥32 weeks) — early-onset PE not significantly different despite higher rate in FRSA group; late-onset PE significantly elevated. Similarly, FGR subdivided by gestational age at first diagnosis (cutoff 32 weeks): early-onset FGR significantly elevated (OR 8.36), late-onset FGR not. Placenta accreta (OR 5.49) and increta/percreta (OR 7.27) significantly elevated. All inclusion criteria required exclusion of known causes of abortion (unexplained FRSA only) — important methodological strength limiting confounding by known risk factors. No intrauterine fetal death occurred in either group. The study's focus on FRSA (specifically first-trimester, unexplained) is more specific than most other studies in this collection.
