Group A: Study Characteristics
AuthorsTC Li, BHK Yip, X Chen
Year2023
JournalHong Kong Medical Journal (Hong Kong Med J 2023;29(Suppl 1):S44–6)
CountryHong Kong SAR, China
Data sourceClinical Data Analysis and Reporting System (CDARS), Hong Kong — electronic medical records from the Department of Obstetrics and Gynaecology, Prince of Wales Hospital, The Chinese University of Hong Kong
Study designRetrospective cohort study (database study)
Study periodJanuary 2000 – December 2019 (20-year period)
Group B: Population
Total N111,124 women with singleton pregnancies
N RM group3,112 (2.8%) with ≥2 miscarriages; primary RM 697 (22.4%); secondary RM 2,415 (77.6%)
N control group108,012 (97.2%) without history of RM
Inclusion criteriaAll women with singleton pregnancy admitted between January 2000 – December 2019 to the Department of Obstetrics and Gynaecology, Prince of Wales Hospital, CUHK. RM group underwent full workup (karyotyping, hormone profiling, prothrombotic screening, antiphospholipid antibodies, thyroid tests, ultrasonography).
Exclusion criteriaMultiple pregnancies excluded
Maternal age RM group<20 yrs: 0.7%, 20–29 yrs: 22.2%, 30–39 yrs: 63.2%, >40 yrs: 13.5%. Significantly older than controls (p < 0.001).
Maternal age control<20 yrs: 5.1%, 20–29 yrs: 35.0%, 30–39 yrs: 53.0%, >40 yrs: 6.9%
BMI RM groupMean ± SD: 22.3 ± 3.8 kg/m²
BMI controlMean ± SD: 22.9 ± 4.2 kg/m² (p = 0.058; not significantly different)
ParityPrimary RM (no prior viable pregnancy): 697 (22.4%); secondary RM (≥1 prior viable pregnancy): 2,415 (77.6%)
ART/IVF rateAssisted conception: 560/3,112 (18.0%) RM vs 11,080/108,012 (10.0%) controls (p < 0.001)
SmokingNR
Group C: Exposure & Control Definition
RM threshold≥2 miscarriages (ESHRE 2017 guideline definition)
Type of lossesHistory of two or more miscarriages; specific type (biochemical vs. clinical, consecutive vs. non-consecutive) not explicitly stated. All women underwent standardized investigation protocol.
Gestational age cutoff"Before the 24th week of gestation" (stated in Introduction)
Dose-responseNo — binary comparison; subgroup by primary vs. secondary RM type
Control groupAll other women without RM history with singleton pregnancies at same institution during same period (n = 108,012)
Group D: Outcomes & Methods
Outcomes measuredGestational hypertension; preeclampsia; eclampsia; antepartum haemorrhage; gestational diabetes; operative vaginal delivery; caesarean section; preterm birth; SGA; LGA; Apgar <7 at 1 min; Apgar <7 at 5 min; neonatal unit admission; perinatal death (all causes); congenital anomalies
Effect measureOR and adjusted OR (95% CI)
Statistical methodStudent's t-test, ANOVA, or non-parametric test; chi-squared or Fisher's exact; univariable and multivariable stepwise logistic regression; SPSS version 25
Confounders adjustedMaternal age, type of pregnancy (natural vs. assisted conception), and gestational age at booking

Group F: Quality Notes
Data source qualityModerate-high — large dataset (N > 111,000) from single tertiary hospital; 20-year data span; CDARS electronic records with high coding accuracy validated by individual record review. Tertiary referral centre: control group may be skewed toward higher pregnancy risk.
Confounder adjustmentPartial — adjusted for maternal age, type of pregnancy (natural vs. assisted conception), and gestational age at booking only. BMI (not significantly different) not adjusted; other potential confounders not adjusted.
Outcome validationICD-9 Clinical Modification (CM) coding; data validation by reviewing individual electronic medical records demonstrated high coding accuracy
Bias acknowledgedPrince of Wales Hospital is a tertiary referral centre (control group may be higher risk, underestimating true differences); predominantly Chinese population (limited generalizability); retrospective design with potentially missing clinical parameters
Other notesAbridged secondary publication (conference supplement — Hong Kong Med J Suppl 1); full report from Health and Medical Research Fund (HMRF) website (project 07180226). Same research group (TC Li) as Jivraj 2001 but uses much larger CDARS database. Uses ESHRE ≥2 miscarriage definition. Four significant adjusted outcomes: caesarean section, preterm birth, SGA, and perinatal death.
Abbreviations: RM = recurrent miscarriage; mc = miscarriage; OR = odds ratio; aOR = adjusted odds ratio; CI = confidence interval; NR = not reported; CDARS = Clinical Data Analysis and Reporting System; CUHK = Chinese University of Hong Kong; SGA = small for gestational age; LGA = large for gestational age; ART = assisted reproductive technology; ICD = International Classification of Diseases; ESHRE = European Society of Human Reproduction and Embryology.
