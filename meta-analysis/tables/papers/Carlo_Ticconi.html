Inclusion criteriaRM group: women with RPL per ESHRE 2017 (≥2 spontaneous losses before 24 wks) attending RPL Units; subsequently became pregnant. Controls: normal healthy pregnant women with ≥2 prior term deliveries, no prior pregnancy losses, no pre-existing medical conditions, no prior gynecologic surgery, no drugs before pregnancy; selected consecutively in Low-Risk Obstetric Clinics.
Exclusion criteriaPre-existing diabetes and hypertension; multiple pregnancies; for controls: pre-existing medical conditions, previous gynecologic surgery, drug use before pregnancy

Exposure & Control Definition
RM threshold≥2 pregnancy losses (ESHRE 2017 Guidelines); study included 2 losses (48.5%) and ≥3 losses (51.5%)
Type of lossesSpontaneous pregnancy loss before 24 weeks; both explained and unexplained RPL included
GA cutoff for miscarriageBefore 24 weeks of gestation (ESHRE 2017 definition)
Dose-response analysisYes — women stratified by number of losses: 2 (n=209), 3, and >3. OR for ≥3 losses vs. 2 losses (overall complications): OR 1.269 (95% CI 1.112–2.386), p < 0.02. Table 4 reports ORs for each specific complication by tier vs. controls.
Control group definitionNormal healthy pregnant women with ≥2 prior uncomplicated term pregnancies, no prior pregnancy losses, no pre-existing medical conditions; followed in Low-Risk Obstetric Clinics of the same institutions
Outcomes & Methods
All outcomes measuredThreatened miscarriage, spontaneous miscarriage, cervical insufficiency, chromosomal/genetic abnormalities, fetal anomalies, oligohydramnios, polyhydramnios, fetal growth restriction, intrauterine fetal death (≥24 wks), GDM, preeclampsia, placenta previa/low-lying placenta, abruptio placentae, pregnancy-related liver disorders (hyperemesis gravidarum, intrahepatic cholestasis, acute fatty liver, HELLP, isolated abnormal liver enzymes, biliary obstruction), preterm PROM, multiple concomitant complications
Effect measureOR (odds ratio)
Statistical methodChi-square test; Student's t-test; Bravais–Pearson correlation; factorial analysis for age and BMI effects (method of analysis of averages). Crude ORs presented; no multivariable regression.
Confounders adjustedNo formal multivariable regression. Age and BMI effects assessed via factorial analysis (Δ% method) — reports the magnitude of age/BMI contributions to each complication separately.

Quality Notes
Data source qualityProspective observational design at two specialized university RPL units; controls from low-risk obstetric clinics of same institutions. Preconceived data template and computerized database; data anonymized before analysis.
Confounder adjustmentPartial — no formal multivariate regression. Age significantly higher in RPL group (35.83 vs. 31.70 yrs) and BMI higher (24.51 vs. 23.41 kg/m²). Factorial analysis (Δ% method) assesses age and BMI effects on each complication separately, but does not constitute formal adjustment.
Outcome validationClinical diagnosis at two university hospitals using ESHRE/ACOG/RCOG/WHO criteria (as specified in Table 1 definitions). Not ICD-coded.
Bias acknowledgedYes — authors acknowledge age and BMI higher in RPL group; factorial analysis quantifies effects. Loss to follow-up: 13.7% of RPL women, 12.1% of controls. Incomplete postnatal genetic follow-up noted. RPL women in specialized units vs. controls in low-risk clinics (care differential). Control group required ≥2 prior term deliveries (parity mismatch with RPL group).
Other notesDose-response by number of losses is a key strength. Subgroup analyses by explained/unexplained and primary/secondary RPL performed. Control group selection (≥2 prior uncomplicated term pregnancies) means controls are inherently lower risk than general obstetric population, potentially inflating ORs. Live birth rates: 86% RPL vs. 97.2% controls.
