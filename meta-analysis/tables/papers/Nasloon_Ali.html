Ali et al., 2020

International Journal of Women's Health — United Arab Emirates (Al Ain) — Prospective cohort (Mutaba'ah Study) — N = 1,737 (gravidity ≥2 subset)
Group A: Study Characteristics
AuthorsNasloon Ali, Iffat Elbarazi, Saad Ghazal-Aswad, Fatima Al-Maskari, Rami H Al-Rifai, Abderrahim Oulhaj, Tom Loney, Luai A Ahmed
Year2020
JournalInternational Journal of Women's Health (2020:12, pp. 1171–1179)
CountryUnited Arab Emirates (Al Ain, UAE)
Data sourceMutaba'ah Mother and Child Health Study — prospective cohort of Emirati pregnant women from three major health institutions in Al Ain (Al Ain Hospital, Tawam Hospital, and a third site). Interim analysis.
Study designProspective cohort study (interim analysis)
Study periodRecruitment May 2017 – April 2019; follow-up until delivery
Group B: Population
Total N1,737 pregnant women with gravidity ≥2 prior to current pregnancy (subset of 2,769 total Mutaba'ah participants)
N RM group234 (13.5%) with history of ≥2 consecutive miscarriages
N control group1,503 (86.5%) with gravidity ≥2 and no history of ≥2 consecutive losses
Inclusion criteriaEmirati pregnant women aged ≥18 years, resident in Al Ain, able to provide informed consent, with gravidity ≥2 prior to current pregnancy
Exclusion criteriaPrimiparous women (n=542) and women with only one previous pregnancy (gravida=1, n=490) excluded from this analysis
Maternal age RM groupMean ± SD: 33.7 ± 5.6 years
Maternal age controlMean ± SD: 32.6 ± 5.2 years (p = 0.005)
BMI RM groupBody weight at first visit: 72.9 ± 14.3 kg (p = 0.479 vs. controls, not significantly different). BMI not separately reported.
BMI controlBody weight: 72.1 ± 14.7 kg
ParityRM: mean children 3.3 ± 0.1; gravidity 6.2 ± 2.6. Controls: mean children 3.4 ± 0.05; gravidity 4.0 ± 2.0. Gravidity significantly higher in RM group (p < 0.001); parity not different (p = 0.278).
ART/IVF ratePrevious infertility treatment: 22.1% (RM) vs 8.1% (controls), p < 0.001. ART in current pregnancy: confidential, unavailable.
SmokingCollected but specific rates not reported per group
Group C: Exposure & Control Definition
RM thresholdPrimary: ≥2 consecutive miscarriages (ESHRE/ACOG/ASRM). Supplementary analysis also performed using ≥3 consecutive losses (76 women from gravidity ≥3 subset).
Type of lossesConsecutive miscarriages; defined as "all pregnancy losses from the time of conception (fertilized ovum) to neonate" including missed miscarriages, ectopic pregnancies, molar pregnancies, or therapeutic abortions. Medical records used to confirm exposure.
Gestational age cutoffBefore the 20th week of gestation
Dose-responseNo — binary threshold in primary analysis. Supplementary analysis with ≥3 losses.
Control groupWomen with gravidity ≥2 and no history of ≥2 consecutive miscarriages (n = 1,503)
Group D: Outcomes & Methods
Outcomes measuredCaesarean section; preterm delivery (<37 weeks); very preterm delivery (<32 weeks); preeclampsia; gestational diabetes mellitus (GDM); blood loss during delivery (mL)
Effect measureCrude OR and adjusted aOR (95% CI)
Statistical methodUnivariate and multivariate logistic regression; Student's t-test (continuous), Pearson Chi-square (categorical); Stata 16
Confounders adjustedAll models adjusted for age, gravidity, body mass (weight at first visit), and gestational age at delivery. Preterm delivery model excluded gestational age at delivery due to collinearity.

Group F: Quality Notes
Data source qualityModerate-high — prospective cohort with medical record extraction; representative of Emirati pregnant population in Al Ain (three major health institutions). Voluntary cohort (response bias possible). Relatively small RM group (n = 234).
Confounder adjustmentPartial — adjusted for age, gravidity, body weight, gestational age at delivery. Unable to adjust for ART in current pregnancy (data confidential), RM etiology, or uterine anomalies.
Outcome validationHospital medical records; outcomes extracted by data collectors; no ICD codes specifically mentioned
Bias acknowledgedInability to adjust for ART in current pregnancy; no RM etiology categorization; potential selection bias (voluntary cohort); interim analysis (incomplete register of miscarriages). Residual confounding by thrombophilia, uterine anomalies, PCOS, thyroid disorders.
Other notesOnly study in the collection from the Middle East (UAE); only study in an Emirati population. High prevalence of consanguinity (44–47%) and GDM (24–26%). Supplementary analysis (≥3 losses) showed similar results, supporting robustness across RM definitions.
Abbreviations: RM = recurrent miscarriage; mc = miscarriage; OR = odds ratio; aOR = adjusted odds ratio; CI = confidence interval; NR = not reported; GDM = gestational diabetes mellitus; CS = caesarean section; ART = assisted reproductive technology; PCOS = polycystic ovary syndrome; ESHRE = European Society of Human Reproduction and Embryology; ACOG = American College of Obstetricians and Gynecologists; ASRM = American Society for Reproductive Medicine.
