JournalAmerican Journal of Obstetrics and Gynecology (Am J Obstet Gynecol 1992;166:111–6)
CountryUnited States (Washington State)
Data sourceWashington State birth certificate records, 1984–1987. Obstetric ward clerks abstracted information from medical charts into birth certificate forms. Checklist-based; reporting completeness ≥80% for most birth complications; congenital malformations underreported (32–57% of hospital discharge diagnoses).
Study designRetrospective cohort (population-based, birth registry)
Study period1984–1987 (birth certificates); index birth year 1987 for 1-SAB and control groups; 1984–1987 for ≥3-SAB group
Population
Total N5,883 (638 RM + 2,146 one-SAB + 3,099 control)
N RM group638 (women with ≥3 prior spontaneous abortions and no other prior pregnancies)
N control group3,099 (primigravid women — no prior pregnancies — randomly selected from 1987 birth records)
Inclusion criteriaRM group: all women with a live singleton birth 1984–1987 with ≥3 prior SABs and no other prior pregnancies. Control group: randomly selected women with a live singleton birth in 1987 with no prior pregnancies (primigravid). One-SAB group (n=2,146): women with exactly one prior SAB and no other prior pregnancies, live singleton birth 1987. Births ending in fetal death excluded (<0.7%).
Exclusion criteriaFetal deaths (<0.7% of pregnancies >20 weeks). Women with prior pregnancies other than SABs excluded from exposed groups.

Maternal age (control)Mean 23.9 years; <20 yr 22.8%; 20–24 yr 34.8%; 25–29 yr 26.3%; >30 yr 16.0%

ParityAll groups primigravid in terms of prior live births (by design). Controls: strictly primigravid. Exposed: prior SABs only, no other pregnancies.
ART/IVF rateNR
Smoking≥3 SABs: 36.5%; one-SAB: 28.1%; control: 19.7% (p < 0.001 after controlling for age)
Exposure & Control Definition
RM threshold≥3 prior spontaneous abortions (primary comparison); also 1 prior SAB group reported separately
Type of lossesSpontaneous abortion as defined on Washington State birth certificate (fetal loss before 20 weeks; first- and second-trimester SABs not differentiated)
GA cutoff for miscarriageBefore 20 weeks' gestation (birth certificate definition)
Dose-response analysisYes — three groups: 0 SABs (control), 1 SAB, ≥3 SABs. Separate adjusted RRs for each group in Table III. Within ≥3-SAB group, dose-response by severity of PTB: 34–36 wks, 30–33 wks, <30 wks.
Control group definition3,099 randomly selected primigravid women with a live singleton birth in 1987 with no pregnancies prior to the index pregnancy
Outcomes & Methods
All outcomes measuredLow birth weight (<2500 g), IUGR (birth weight <10th percentile for GA; Brenner nomogram), preterm birth (<37 wks; subgroups: 34–36, 30–33, <30 wks), congenital malformations (any, excluding ductus arteriosus), placenta previa, prolonged ROM (>24 hrs), breech presentation, Apgar 1 min <6, Apgar 5 min <7, preeclampsia, abruptio placentae
Effect measureRR (relative risk), estimated via maximum likelihood logistic regression
Statistical methodMaximum likelihood logistic regression (Breslow & Day method)
Confounders adjustedMaternal age and smoking (for LBW, IUGR, GA, preeclampsia); maternal age and gestational length (for placenta previa, prolonged ROM, breech); maternal age, smoking, gestational length (for Apgar scores). Race, marital status, urban residence, prenatal care, SES evaluated but not retained.

Quality Notes
Data source qualityLarge population-based birth certificate registry (Washington State). Advantage: large N, population-based, standardized data collection. Limitation: birth certificates underreport complications (congenital malformations 32–57% captured; most other complications ≥80%). SAB history taken from medical records before birth outcome recorded, reducing differential misclassification.
Confounder adjustmentPartial — adjusted for maternal age and smoking (primary confounders). BMI not available. Gestational age used as additional confounder for selected outcomes. Race, marital status, urban residence, prenatal care found not to confound.
Outcome validationBirth certificate checklist format; completeness ≥80% for most outcomes; congenital malformations substantially underreported (32–57%). No ICD codes.
Bias acknowledgedYes — (1) misclassification of SAB count from birth certificates (non-differential, biases toward null); (2) possible association with unmeasured variables (nutrition, alcohol/drug abuse); (3) birth certificates do not distinguish first- vs. second-trimester SABs; (4) small numbers limit subgroup analyses.
Other notesKey design strength: all exposed women had ONLY prior SABs and no other prior pregnancies, controlling for parity confounding. Control group is strictly primigravid. Primary comparison of interest: ≥3 SABs vs. 0 SABs.
