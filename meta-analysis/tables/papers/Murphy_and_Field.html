Field & Murphy, 2015

Human Reproduction — Ireland (Dublin, Coombe Hospital) — Retrospective cohort — N = 30,053 — Comparison group includes women with ≤2 mc
Group A: Study Characteristics
AuthorsK. Field, D.J. Murphy
Year2015
JournalHuman Reproduction (Vol. 30, No. 5, pp. 1239–1245)
CountryIreland (Dublin)
Data sourceElectronic antenatal and delivery records from the Coombe Women and Infants University Hospital, Dublin — large urban university-affiliated maternity hospital delivering 8,500–9,000 women per year. Data from first hospital visit, detailed booking interview, delivery, postnatal discharge, and neonatal records.
Study designRetrospective cohort study
Study periodJanuary 2008 – July 2011 (approximately 3.5 years)
Group B: Population
Total N30,053 women with singleton pregnancies
N RM group2,030 (6.8%) with ≥3 miscarriages; primary RM 382 (18.8%); secondary RM 1,648 (81.2%)
N control group28,023 (93.2%) without ≥3 miscarriages (includes women with 0, 1, or 2 prior miscarriages)
Inclusion criteriaAll women with singleton pregnancies booked at the Coombe Women and Infants University Hospital between January 2008 – July 2011. Records available for all deliveries >23 weeks or ≥500 g.
Exclusion criteriaMultiple pregnancies; women who attended antenatally but delivered elsewhere
Maternal age RM group>40 yrs: 12.0% (n=243); 30–39 yrs: 67.1% (n=1,362); 20–29 yrs: 20.3% (n=413); <20 yrs: 0.6% (n=12). Significantly older than controls.
Maternal age control>40 yrs: 3.7%; 30–39 yrs: 53.6%; 20–29 yrs: 38.9%; <20 yrs: 3.8%
BMI RM groupBMI ≥30 (obese): 21.9% (n=429) vs 14.6% controls (p < 0.05); BMI <18: 0.7% vs 1.2%
BMI controlBMI ≥30: 14.6% (n=4,085)
ParityNulliparous (primary RM): 382/2,030 (18.8%); parous (secondary RM): 1,648/2,030 (81.2%). Controls: nulliparous 42.8%. RM women significantly less likely to be nulliparous (OR 0.31, 95% CI 0.28–0.35).
ART/IVF rateAssisted conception: 85/2,030 (4.2%) RM vs 653/28,023 (2.3%) controls (OR 1.83, 95% CI 1.45–2.31)
SmokingCurrent smoker: 348/2,030 (17.1%) RM vs 4,494/28,023 (16.0%) controls (OR 1.08; not significant)
Group C: Exposure & Control Definition
RM threshold≥3 miscarriages (not necessarily consecutive). Note: "the strict definition of recurrent miscarriage is three consecutive miscarriages and while each woman in the study group had three or more miscarriages, they were only confirmed to be consecutive in the primary RM group."
Type of lossesThree or more previous miscarriages regardless of whether consecutive and regardless of partner; not sub-classified by aetiology
Gestational age cutoffNot explicitly stated; WHO definition cited; viability at 23 weeks at this hospital
Dose-responseNo — single threshold (≥3 mc) vs. comparison group; subgroup by primary vs. secondary RM
Control groupAll women without ≥3 miscarriages (includes 0, 1, or 2 prior mc) (n = 28,023). NOTE: women with 2 miscarriages are included in the comparison group — this likely underestimates the true effect size.
Group D: Outcomes & Methods
Outcomes measuredGestational age at delivery (mean); birthweight (mean); preterm birth <37 wks; very preterm birth <32 wks; SGA (<10th centile, individualized); male infant; Apgar <7 at 5 min; neonatal unit admission; congenital abnormality; aneuploidy; perinatal death; pre-eclampsia/PIH; abruption/APH; placenta praevia; scheduled (elective) CS; emergency CS; spontaneous vaginal delivery; operative vaginal delivery; spontaneous PTB <37 wks
Effect measureOR for categorical outcomes; difference in means (95% CI) for continuous outcomes; adjusted OR (adjOR) from multivariable logistic regression
Statistical methodSPSS version 15; univariable and multivariable stepwise logistic regression; results as proportions, ORs, and 95% CIs
Confounders adjustedMaternal age, BMI, marital status, socio-economic group, parity, smoking, medical disorders, psychiatric disorders, assisted conception, previous perinatal death

Group F: Quality Notes
Data source qualityHigh — large complete electronic dataset from single university maternity hospital; very little missing data; accurate gestational age dating; routine ascertainment of perinatal outcomes; neonatal records available until discharge
Confounder adjustmentYes — multivariable stepwise logistic regression adjusting for maternal age, BMI, marital status, socio-economic group, parity, smoking, medical disorders, psychiatric disorders, assisted conception, previous perinatal death. More complete adjustment than most studies in this collection.
Outcome validationElectronic records with clinical definitions; SGA uses individualized birthweight ratios (gestation.net) corrected for multiple maternal and infant characteristics — more sophisticated SGA definition than most other studies. Perinatal death: stillbirth (≥24 wks) + early neonatal death (within 7 days; more restrictive than 28-day definition used elsewhere).
Bias acknowledgedRetrospective design; miscarriages not all confirmed consecutive (except primary RM group); women not categorized by aetiology; women with 2 miscarriages in control group may attenuate true association; potential recall errors for order of prior pregnancies
Other notesLargest Irish hospital-based cohort in the collection. NOTE: Women with 2 miscarriages are in the comparison group — this compares ≥3 mc vs ≤2 mc rather than ≥3 mc vs 0 mc; this likely underestimates true effect size. The individualized SGA definition may explain why SGA is not significant here while it is in other studies. Adjusted perinatal death OR (2.66) notably higher than crude (2.26). TC Li is cited (Jivraj 2001) as prior work. Same RM definition (≥3 mc).
Abbreviations: RM = recurrent miscarriage; mc = miscarriage; OR = odds ratio; aOR = adjusted odds ratio; CI = confidence interval; NR = not reported; PTB = preterm birth; SGA = small for gestational age; PIH = pregnancy-induced hypertension; APH = antepartum haemorrhage; CS = caesarean section; ART = assisted reproductive technology; BMI = body mass index.
