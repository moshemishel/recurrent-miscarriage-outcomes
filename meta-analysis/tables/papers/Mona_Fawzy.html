Fawzy et al., 2016

Human Fertility — United Kingdom (Sheffield) — Retrospective case-control — N = 40,260 (400 RM + 39,860 controls) — Crude ORs only (Mantel-Haenszel)
Group A: Study Characteristics
AuthorsMona Fawzy, Sotirios Saravelos, Tin Chiu Li, Mostafa Metwally
Year2016
JournalHuman Fertility (Vol. 19, No. 1, pp. 9–15)
CountryUnited Kingdom (Sheffield, England)
Data sourceTwo hospital databases: (1) RM clinic database, Jessop Wing, Royal Hallamshire Hospital (prospectively collected); (2) general obstetric database (Protos evolution) at the same hospital, including all births at hospital and home
Study designRetrospective case-control study (analysis of prospectively collected data)
Study periodJanuary 2001 – July 2007
Group B: Population
Total N40,260 (400 RM + 39,860 controls)
N RM group400 consecutive women with history of RM who achieved pregnancies progressing beyond 24 weeks
N control group39,860 deliveries from the general obstetric database during the same period
Inclusion criteriaRM group: attended RM clinic at Jessop Wing; achieved pregnancies progressing beyond 24 weeks; January 2001 – July 2007. Control group: all births in hospital database same period (including home births); excluding babies from other units transferred to SCBU and twins.
Exclusion criteriaTwins and babies admitted to SCBU from other units excluded from both groups
Maternal age RM group32.6 ± 5.7 years (mean ± SD); secondary RM older than primary (34.0 ± 5.4 vs 31.7 ± 5.7 years)
Maternal age control29.0 ± 6.2 years (significantly younger, p < 0.0001)
BMI RM groupMedian 24.1 (range 16.1–49.9) kg/m²; primary RM median 23.6; secondary RM median 29.1
BMI controlNR
Parity143 (36%) primary miscarriage (no prior live birth); 257 (64%) secondary miscarriage (≥1 prior live birth)
ART/IVF rateNR
SmokingNR
Group C: Exposure & Control Definition
RM threshold≥3 consecutive spontaneous miscarriages before 20 weeks gestation
Type of lossesThree or more consecutive spontaneous miscarriages; APS cases treated with enoxaparin and aspirin included; uterine anomalies treated surgically included; full investigative protocol required
Gestational age cutoffBefore 20 weeks gestation
Dose-responseNo — single threshold (≥3 mc) vs. control; subgroup analyses by type (primary vs. secondary RM, unexplained RM, fetal sex)
Control groupAll deliveries from general obstetric database (Protos evolution) at same hospital during same period (n = 39,860); includes all births at hospital and home
Group D: Outcomes & Methods
Outcomes measuredAntepartum haemorrhage (APH); gestational diabetes; pregnancy induced hypertension (PIH); preterm labour <37 wks; emergency caesarean section; foetal sex ratio; intrapartum foetal distress; SGA (<2 SD below mean); SCBU admission; stillbirth (≥24 wks); neonatal death (≤28 days); perinatal death; Apgar <6 at 1 minute; Apgar <6 at 5 minutes
Effect measureOR (odds ratio) with 95% CI — Mantel-Haenszel fixed-effect method (RevMan software)
Statistical methodChi-squared or Mann-Whitney for group comparisons; OR and 95% CI via RevMan (Mantel-Haenszel fixed-effect); no multivariable regression — ORs are unadjusted
Confounders adjustedNone in primary analysis (crude ORs only); subgroup analyses address some confounders (primary vs. secondary RM; unexplained RM; sex of fetus)

Group F: Quality Notes
Data source qualityModerate — RM group data collected prospectively as part of clinic care; control data from hospital obstetric database; both from same institution. Hospital is a large tertiary referral centre (may skew control population toward higher risk, which would minimize differences).
Confounder adjustmentNo — primary analysis uses crude ORs only (Mantel-Haenszel); subgroup analyses partially address parity and aetiology confounding. No multivariable regression. Age not adjusted (RM group significantly older, p < 0.0001).
Outcome validationHospital database records; clinical definitions specified for each outcome; no ICD coding mentioned
Bias acknowledgedTertiary referral centre may skew RM group toward more complex pathologies; ethnicity data not available; higher proportion of secondary vs. primary RM; age difference not adjusted
Other notesFollow-on study from Jivraj et al. 2001 (same centre, same RM definition); larger RM cohort (400 vs. 162). Subgroup analysis showing male fetus association with worse outcomes is unique in this collection. No adjustment for age, BMI, or parity is a significant limitation. Uses ≥3 consecutive miscarriages threshold (older UK definition).
Abbreviations: RM = recurrent miscarriage; mc = miscarriage; OR = odds ratio; CI = confidence interval; NR = not reported; APH = antepartum haemorrhage; PIH = pregnancy induced hypertension; SGA = small for gestational age; SCBU = special care baby unit; APS = antiphospholipid syndrome; PTB = preterm birth.
