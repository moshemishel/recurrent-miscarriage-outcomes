Population
Total N492 singleton pregnant women (164 FRSA + 328 controls); from 12,811 total singleton deliveries during study period
N RM group164 (1.28% of total deliveries)
N control group328 (randomly selected 1:2 from same period deliveries without FRSA)
Inclusion criteriaFRSA group: ≥2 abortions during first trimester; normal chromosome karyotypes (both partners); no abnormal reproductive tract anatomy; normal thyroid/endocrine/insulin; TORCH IgM negative; antiphospholipid and antinuclear antibodies negative; normal coagulation; negative cervical secretion; negative infectious diseases; blocking antibodies not detected. Only unexplained FRSA included. Controls: women without FRSA history; randomly selected 1:2.
Exclusion criteriaMedical complications (chronic hypertension, chronic kidney disease, autoimmune diseases, severe late-term complications transferred from other hospitals); women with known causes of abortion
Maternal age (RM)33.97 ± 4.25 years (mean ± SD)

BMI (RM)23.8 ± 3.3 kg/m²

Exposure & Control Definition
RM threshold≥2 abortions during early pregnancy (first trimester; FRSA — first-trimester recurrent spontaneous abortion)
Type of lossesUnexplained first-trimester losses only (within 12 weeks); women with known causes excluded
GA cutoff for miscarriageFirst trimester (within 12 weeks of pregnancy)
Dose-response analysisNo — only ≥2 FRSA vs. no FRSA
Control group definitionRandomly selected at 1:2 ratio from women delivering at same hospital during same period without FRSA history
Outcomes & Methods
All outcomes measuredCesarean section, postpartum hemorrhage; early-onset PE (<32 wks), late-onset PE (≥32 wks); oligohydramnios; early-onset FGR (<32 wks), late-onset FGR (≥32 wks); placental abruption; late abortion (second-trimester); PTB (<37 wks); very PTB (<32 wks); placenta previa; placenta accreta; placenta increta and percreta; SGA; perinatal death; neonatal severe asphyxia; birth defects; gestational age at delivery; birth weight
Effect measureOR (crude) and adjusted OR (multivariate logistic regression for selected outcomes)
Statistical methodIndependent sample t-test (continuous); chi-square (categorical); multivariate logistic regression with backward elimination (SPSS version 19.0)
Confounders adjustedMaternal age adjusted for: PE (combined), oligohydramnios, late abortion, CS (Tables 4 and 6). No comprehensive multivariable adjustment for all outcomes.

Quality Notes
Data source qualitySingle tertiary university hospital (regional referral center); computerized perinatal database with quality controls (two named authors reviewed data before entry). Peking University Third Hospital is a university-affiliated high-class hospital and regional intensive care referral center; controls may also represent higher-risk pregnancies. Sample size relatively small (164 vs. 328).
Confounder adjustmentPartial — multivariate logistic regression for selected outcomes (PE, oligohydramnios, late abortion, CS). Significant confounders: maternal age (33.97 vs. 30.48 yrs, p < 0.001); gravidity (3.9 vs. 2.2, p < 0.001). BMI and parity were not significantly different.
Outcome validationPhysician-coded from medical records and perinatal database; clinical diagnosis using specified cutoffs (e.g., 32 weeks for early/late FGR and PE classification). No ICD coding mentioned.
Bias acknowledgedYes — (1) sample selection bias in retrospective study; (2) incomplete exclusion of abortion causes possible; (3) control group also high-risk (referral hospital); (4) small numbers for some outcomes; (5) FVL/coagulation factor testing not routinely performed in Chinese Han population.
Other notesUnique: subdivides PE into early-onset (<32 wks, NS) and late-onset (≥32 wks, significant). Similarly subdivides FGR by GA at diagnosis. Placenta accreta (OR 5.49) and increta/percreta (OR 7.27) significantly elevated. All inclusion criteria required exclusion of known causes (unexplained FRSA only) — key methodological strength. No IUFD occurred in either group.
