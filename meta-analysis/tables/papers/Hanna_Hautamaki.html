JournalActa Obstetricia et Gynecologica Scandinavica
CountryFinland (Helsinki)
Data sourceHospital medical records (Helsinki University Hospital RPL referrals) linked with Finnish Medical Birth Register (MBR; covers all Finnish births since 1987; physician-coded ICD-10 diagnoses); data linkage via pseudonymized personal identity numbers through Findata
Study designRetrospective case-control study (1:4 matched)
Study periodRPL examinations 2007–2016; follow-up until December 31, 2019
Population
Total N1,800 (360 RPL cases + 1,440 controls)
N RM group360 (first singleton birth after RPL investigations; 71.1% of 506 referred couples had singleton birth)
N control group1,440 (4 controls per case matched from MBR)
Inclusion criteriaCases: couples referred to Helsinki University Hospital for RPL 2007–2016; first pregnancy after RPL investigations ending in singleton birth. Most studied after ≥3 consecutive clinical first-trimester losses OR ≥2 losses with ≥1 in second trimester (12+1–21+6 GWs) when age <42 years. Controls: 4 controls per RPL woman matched from MBR for age, parity, year/month of delivery, hospital. Controls with ≥3 miscarriages (n=27) excluded from sub-analyses.
Exclusion criteriaMultiple pregnancies; non-first subsequent pregnancy after RPL investigations
Maternal age (RM)Mean 34.4 years (SD 4.85); <25: 4.2%; 25–29: 14.2%; 30–34: 35.8%; 35–39: 33.6%; ≥40: 12.2%
Maternal age (control)Mean 34.4 years (SD 4.84) — matched by design; p = 1.00
BMI (RM)Mean 24.43 kg/m² (SD 4.43); <20: 13.1%; 20–24.9: 49.7%; 25–29.9: 22.8%; ≥30: 12.5%; unknown: 1.9%
BMI (control)Mean 24.09 kg/m² (SD 4.65); p = 0.19 (not significantly different)
ParityMatched by design: primipara 163 RPL / 652 controls; multipara 197 RPL / 788 controls
ART/IVF rateRPL: 13.1% any ART; controls: 9.9%; p = 0.01
SmokingCurrent smoker: RPL 4.7%, controls 5.4%; p = 0.34 (not significant)
Exposure & Control Definition
RM threshold≥2 losses (ESHRE); in practice ≥3 consecutive first-trimester losses OR ≥2 losses with ≥1 in second trimester (12+1–21+6 GWs) when age <42 years
Type of lossesConsecutive and non-consecutive; clinical first-trimester losses; also second-trimester losses. Both explained and unexplained RPL included.
GA cutoff for miscarriageBefore 22+0 gestational weeks
Dose-response analysisNo formal dose-response. Sub-analyses: secondary RPL (n=197) vs. multipara controls (n=788); unexplained RPL (n=228) vs. explained RPL (n=132).
Control group definitionFour women from MBR per RPL woman, matched for age, parity, year/month of delivery, and hospital of delivery
Outcomes & Methods
All outcomes measuredGestational hypertension (O13), preeclampsia (O14), GDM (O24.4), PTB (<37 GWs), malpresentation (O32), PROM (O42), placental problems composite (O43/O44/O45 + manual removal), manual removal of placenta, placenta previa (O44), placental abruption (O45), postpartum hemorrhage (O72), induced labor, birthweight <2500 g, mean birthweight, SGA (<−2 SDs Finnish growth curves), umbilical artery pH <7.10, neonatal composite (Apgar 5 min 0–6, NICU, perinatal death, pH <7.10), prematurity composite, P02/P07 diagnoses, Apgar 5 min, NICU, perinatal death, cervical incompetence (O34.3), LMWH prophylaxis
Effect measureRR (risk ratio); OR for GDM (BMI-adjusted logistic regression)
Statistical methodChi-squared (categorical); t-test or Mann–Whitney U (continuous); RRs with 95% CIs for all outcomes; logistic regression for BMI-adjusted GDM. Outcomes with <5 cases per cell not reported (Finnish data protection).
Confounders adjustedBMI adjustment for GDM only (OR 1.33, 95% CI 0.95–1.82 after BMI adjustment, p = 0.10 — became non-significant). All other outcomes: unadjusted RRs. Confounders controlled by matching (age, parity, hospital, delivery time period).

Quality Notes
Data source qualityStrong design: thoroughly investigated RPL couples (clinical data) linked to Finnish MBR (register data). MBR covers all Finnish newborns since 1987; missing/incorrect data double-checked from birth hospitals. Complete follow-up for all 360 RPL women.
Confounder adjustmentMinimal — BMI adjustment for GDM only. All other outcomes crude RRs. Confounding controlled primarily through matching (age, parity, hospital, delivery time period). BMI not significantly different. Smoking similar. Key unmatched confounders: ART use (13.1% vs. 9.9%).
Outcome validationICD-10 coded from MBR; physician-coded; data quality validated. Outcomes with 1–4 cases not reported.
Bias acknowledgedYes — (1) insufficient power for rare outcomes; (2) data protection suppresses outcomes with <5 cases; (3) paternity not confirmed in MBR; (4) GDM screening not uniform before 2008.
Other notesMost recent study in collection (accepted Dec 2024). Key finding: gestational hypertension (not preeclampsia) significantly elevated. GDM elevated crude but lost significance after BMI adjustment. Placental problems composite and manual removal significantly elevated. Secondary RPL SGA more common (RR 2.50). Women with RPL used significantly more LMWH prophylaxis (9.4% vs. 2.4%), more outpatient visits, longer hospital stays.
