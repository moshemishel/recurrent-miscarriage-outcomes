Exposure & Control Definition
RM threshold≥2 previous miscarriages (ESHRE definition: loss of ≥2 pregnancies before 24th week of gestation; consecutive not required)
Type of lossesAny spontaneous miscarriage (not restricted to consecutive); first- and second-trimester combined. Primary RM (≥2 mc, no prior viable pregnancy): n=1,351 (67.75%); secondary RM: n=643 (32.25%).
GA cutoff for miscarriageBefore 24 weeks of gestation (ESHRE definition)
Dose-response analysisNo formal dose-response. Stratified analyses by parity (nulliparous vs. multiparous) and RM type (explained vs. unexplained, primary vs. secondary). Sensitivity analysis with single miscarriage + no miscarriage as combined reference.
Control group definitionAll women with no history of spontaneous miscarriage who delivered at institution; 95,321 women (87.62% of total)
Outcomes & Methods
All outcomes measuredGDM, preeclampsia (overall, preterm PE, term PE), placenta previa, placenta accreta, placental abruption (listed in Methods; not separately tabulated in results — see notes), fetal distress, emergency CS, elective CS, induction, postpartum hemorrhage, PTB (<37 wks), stillbirth (≥28 wks), Apgar <7 (5 min), neonatal asphyxia, congenital malformation, LBW (<2500 g), neonatal death
Effect measureaRR (adjusted risk ratio); log binomial regression (primary); Poisson regression with robust SE when log binomial did not converge
Statistical methodLog binomial regression; Poisson regression; chi-squared/Fisher's exact; interaction term in log binomial to test parity effect modification
Confounders adjustedMaternal age, prepregnancy BMI, marital status, insurance, parity, residence, education, ART, diabetes, chronic hypertension, hypothyroidism, APS. Placenta previa/accreta additionally adjusted for CS history. LBW additionally adjusted for PTB. Missing BMI/education (9.7%) assigned to largest category (main analysis); complete case analysis as sensitivity.

Quality Notes
Data source qualityLargest study in collection (N=108,792) from large tertiary urban maternity hospital (~16,000 annual deliveries); comprehensive electronic antenatal and delivery records with physician-coded ICD-10 diagnoses; neonatal records until hospital discharge. Tertiary center setting influences outcome prevalence.
Confounder adjustmentMost comprehensive in collection — multivariable log binomial regression adjusting for 12 variables: maternal age, BMI, marital status, insurance, parity, residence, education, ART, diabetes, chronic hypertension, hypothyroidism, APS. Sensitivity analyses: unexplained RM vs. no RM; RM vs. combined no-/single-miscarriage reference; complete case analysis.
Outcome validationICD-10 coded by physicians; electronic records system. Preeclampsia subdivided into preterm (placental disorder) vs. term (maternal disorder). Stillbirth restricted to ≥28 weeks per WHO. Missing BMI/education (9.7%) handled by assigning to largest category.
Bias acknowledgedYes — (1) tertiary center influences prevalence; (2) only pregnancies beyond 24 weeks included; (3) first- vs. second-trimester miscarriages not distinguished; (4) exposure based on self-report — possible recall bias; (5) APS and some conditions documented by physician/patient report rather than strict criteria; (6) more detailed documentation in RM patients possible (information bias).
Other notesKey strengths: (1) preeclampsia subdivided into preterm vs. term (only preterm PE significant, aRR 1.58); (2) placenta accreta strongly associated (aRR 4.19); (3) parity as effect modifier formally tested — multiparous RM had stronger associations for PE, emergency CS, postpartum hemorrhage; (4) unexplained RM sensitivity analysis confirms findings for placental outcomes. Placental abruption listed in Methods but not separately tabulated in results.
Abbreviations: aOR = adjusted odds ratio; aRR = adjusted relative risk; aHR = adjusted hazard ratio; ART = assisted reproductive technology; BMI = body mass index; CI = confidence interval; IUGR = intrauterine growth restriction; NR = not reported; OR = odds ratio; PTB = preterm birth; RM = recurrent miscarriage; RR = relative risk (or rate ratio); SGA = small for gestational age; wks = weeks
