Exposure & Control Definition
RM threshold≥3 consecutive miscarriages (traditional Swedish/RCOG definition; ICD-10 N96.9 or O26.2). PPV of RPL diagnosis in NPR: 85% (95% CI 78–89%).
Type of lossesConsecutive miscarriages before viability; ICD-coded diagnoses
GA cutoff for miscarriageBefore viability; fetal death after 22 completed GWs = stillbirth
Dose-response analysisNo — only RPL (≥3 losses) vs. no RPL; subgroup comparing primary vs. secondary RPL
Control group definitionRandom selection of women without RPL diagnosis who had singleton childbirths in MFR 2003–2012 (~5% of all Swedish childbirths). Primary RPL compared with nulliparous reference; secondary RPL compared with multiparous reference.
Outcomes & Methods
All outcomes measuredPre-eclampsia (ICD-10 O14–O15; mild/moderate and severe); stillbirth (≥22 GWs); SGA (<2 SDs below mean; live and stillborn); PTB (<37 GWs; 32–36 GWs; <32 GWs; spontaneous vs. iatrogenic); placental abruption (ICD-10 O45). Each outcome further subdivided into preterm and term presentation.
Effect measureAOR (adjusted odds ratio); note: "since there were low initial risk of the obstetrical outcomes, ORs could be used equivalent to relative risk"
Statistical methodStepwise unconditional multiple logistic regression; univariable ORs also reported; factors with univariable p < 0.2 entered into multiple model
Confounders adjustedOutcome-specific: pre-eclampsia — age, parity, assisted conception, BMI, country of birth, chronic hypertension, pregestational diabetes, smoking; stillbirth — age, parity, BMI, smoking; SGA — age, parity, BMI, smoking, country of birth, chronic hypertension; PTB — age, parity, BMI, smoking; abruption — age, parity, BMI, smoking, chronic hypertension. Missing BMI imputed with mean.

Quality Notes
Data source qualityLarge national register (N=62,381); MFR covers >97% of Swedish births; NPR covers all inpatient since 1987, outpatient since 2001; linked via personal identity numbers. PPV of pre-eclampsia in MFR: 93%. PPV of RPL diagnosis in NPR: 85% (95% CI 78–89%).
Confounder adjustmentYes — multivariable logistic regression with outcome-specific confounder sets. Key confounders: maternal age, BMI, smoking, parity, assisted reproduction, chronic hypertension, pregestational diabetes, country of birth. Missing BMI handled by mean imputation (~10% missing).
Outcome validationICD-10 coded diagnoses (N96.9/O26.2 for RPL; O14–O15 for PE; O45 for abruption). PE PPV validated at 93%.
Bias acknowledgedYes — (1) PPV of RPL diagnosis 85% (~15% false positives diluting toward null); (2) missing BMI ~10% handled by imputation; (3) missing delivery onset data for 4.7% assumed spontaneous; (4) missing SGA data assumed AGA for 313 births; (5) only first subsequent childbirth analyzed; (6) no information on preterm PROM.
Other notesLargest study in this collection. Key strength: primary vs. secondary RPL vs. matched-parity reference groups. Critical finding: placenta-associated disorders strongly associate with preterm birth — outcomes insignificant at term become strongly significant when birth is preterm. RM definition (≥3 losses) stricter than many other studies.
