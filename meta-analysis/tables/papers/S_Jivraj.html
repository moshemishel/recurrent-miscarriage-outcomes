Group A: Study Characteristics
AuthorsS. Jivraj, B. Anstie, Y-C. Cheong, F.M. Fairlie, S.M. Laird, T.C. Li
Year2001
JournalHuman Reproduction (Volume 16, Issue 1, January 2001, Pages 102–106)
CountryUnited Kingdom (Sheffield, England)
Data source(1) RM group: hospital records, RM clinic at Jessop Hospital for Women, Sheffield; (2) Control group: hospital database of all deliveries at Jessop Hospital for Women during same period
Study designRetrospective cohort study
Study period1 January 1992 – 30 June 1998
Group B: Population
Total N24,861 (162 RM + 24,699 controls)
N RM group162 pregnancies (from 178 eligible women; 91% case note retrieval; only first singleton delivery retrieved)
N control group24,699 singleton deliveries
Inclusion criteriaRM group: patients with ≥3 miscarriages attending clinic 1992–1998; had ≥1 singleton pregnancy progressing beyond 24 weeks; only first such pregnancy included. Control group: all deliveries from singleton pregnancies at same hospital during same period.
Exclusion criteriaRM group: multiple pregnancies excluded; 16 patients delivering at district general hospitals excluded. Control group: multiple pregnancies excluded.
Maternal age RM groupMean ± SD: 32.0 ± 5.4 years at conception; mean number of miscarriages 3.4 (range 3–10)
Maternal age controlMean ± SD: 28.3 ± 5.4 years
BMI RM groupNR
BMI controlNR
ParityRM: 95 (59%) primary (no prior live birth); 67 (41%) secondary (≥1 prior live birth); mean parity 0.6 ± 0.8. Controls: mean parity 0.8 ± 1.1.
ART/IVF rateNR
SmokingNR
Group C: Exposure & Control Definition
RM threshold≥3 consecutive spontaneous miscarriages
Type of lossesThree or more consecutive spontaneous miscarriages; etiological subgroups: unknown 28%, unexplained 38%, prothrombotic state 13.5%, structural uterine abnormality 7%, endocrine factors 13.5%, chromosomal anomalies 0%. All underwent comprehensive investigative protocol.
Gestational age cutoffNot explicitly stated; viability defined at 24 weeks (study restricted to deliveries beyond 24 weeks)
Dose-responseNo — single threshold (≥3 mc) vs. control; subgroup analyses by primary vs. secondary miscarriage and unexplained miscarriage
Control groupAll singleton deliveries from same hospital database 1992–1998 (n = 24,699)
Group D: Outcomes & Methods
Outcomes measuredHypertension overall; chronic hypertension; PIH/pre-eclampsia; diabetes; preterm delivery (<37 wks); SGA (<2 SD below mean); caesarean section (overall, emergency, elective); normal vaginal delivery; instrumental delivery; perinatal mortality; SCBU admission
Effect measureProportions/rates compared; no OR or RR calculated — chi-squared test used
Statistical methodChi-squared test for comparisons between study and control group
Confounders adjustedNone — no multivariable regression; age and parity differences acknowledged but not adjusted. Parity addressed via subgroup only.

Group F: Quality Notes
Data source qualityModerate — hospital-based cohort; RM clinic with detailed investigation protocol; case note retrieval rate 91%; controls from same hospital database. 16 RM cases (9%) delivered elsewhere and excluded.
Confounder adjustmentNo — chi-squared only; no multivariable regression. Age difference acknowledged (RM 32.0 vs. control 28.3 yrs) but not adjusted. Parity addressed through subgroup analysis only.
Outcome validationClinical case note review; standardized clinical definitions for all outcomes; SGA per Keen and Pearse (1988) growth charts
Bias acknowledgedAge and parity not matched; 9% case note loss; caesarean section rate in control group increased over study period (12.4% in 1992 to 22.9% in 1998) — temporal confounding
Other notesOne of the most frequently cited studies in the collection; original study from Jessop Wing Sheffield RM clinic. Preceded Fawzy 2016 (same centre, same clinic) by 15 years. Effect measures reported as raw proportions with chi-squared only — no ORs or RRs. TC Li is last/senior author. RM threshold: ≥3 consecutive miscarriages (older UK definition).
Abbreviations: RM = recurrent miscarriage; mc = miscarriage; OR = odds ratio; CI = confidence interval; NR = not reported; PIH = pregnancy-induced hypertension; SGA = small for gestational age; SCBU = special care baby unit; CS = caesarean section; PTB = preterm birth.
