Quality Notes
Data source qualitySingle academic institution perinatal database; detailed medical records; ability to verify GA and consecutiveness of losses (small RM cohort, 1.3% of eligible, reflects strict inclusion criteria). Single center, predominantly Black population (49.4% RM; 49.2% controls).
Confounder adjustmentYes — comprehensive multivariable logistic regression with outcome-specific confounder sets. Key confounders: prior PTB (critical for PTB outcome: 25.1% vs. 9.0%), maternal age, parity (80% parous RM vs. 43.7%), BMI, race, payment, chronic hypertension, pregestational diabetes.
Outcome validationPhysician-recorded institutional perinatal database; GDM by Carpenter–Coustan criteria; SGA by Brenner's nomogram; hypertensive diseases as recorded by delivery physician.
Bias acknowledgedYes — (1) RPL defined by patient self-report (possible misclassification biasing toward null); (2) RM cohort smaller than expected due to strict inclusion criteria; (3) etiology of RPL unknown; (4) multiple comparisons raise possibility of chance finding for GDM; (5) confounders from older maternal age and comorbidities.
Other notesControl group includes women with 0 or 1 prior SAB (dilutes comparison vs. true nulliparous control). RM definition (≥2 consecutive, <12 wks) is narrower than most other studies. Apparently increased crude PTB becomes non-significant after adjustment — highlighting importance of prior PTB as confounder. GDM (aOR 1.69) is the study's main positive finding.
