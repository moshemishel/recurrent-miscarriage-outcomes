# Field & Murphy, 2015 — Meta-Analysis Extraction

## Group A: Study Characteristics
- **Authors**: K. Field, D.J. Murphy
- **Year**: 2015
- **Journal**: Human Reproduction (Vol. 30, No. 5, pp. 1239–1245)
- **Country**: Ireland (Dublin)
- **Data source**: Electronic antenatal and delivery records of all women with singleton pregnancies attending the Coombe Women and Infants University Hospital, Dublin — a large urban university-affiliated maternity hospital delivering 8,500–9,000 women per year. Data collated from the electronic record system including first hospital visit, detailed booking interview, delivery, postnatal discharge, and neonatal records.
- **Study design**: Retrospective cohort study
- **Study period**: January 2008 – July 2011 (approximately 3.5 years)

## Group B: Population
- **Total N**: 30,053 women with singleton pregnancies
- **N RM group**: 2,030 (6.8%) with a history of three or more miscarriages; of these, 382 (18.8%) had primary RM (three or more consecutive miscarriages, no prior viable pregnancy) and 1,648 (81.2%) had secondary RM
- **N control group**: 28,023 (93.2%) without a history of three or more miscarriages (includes women with 0, 1, or 2 prior miscarriages)
- **Inclusion criteria**: All women with singleton pregnancies who booked for antenatal care and delivery at the Coombe Women and Infants University Hospital between January 2008 and July 2011. Comprehensive records for all deliveries >23 weeks gestation or ≥500 g birthweight available.
- **Exclusion criteria**: Multiple pregnancies; women who attended for antenatal care but delivered elsewhere
- **Maternal age RM group**: >40 years: 12.0% (n=243); 30–39 years: 67.1% (n=1,362); 20–29 years: 20.3% (n=413); <20 years: 0.6% (n=12). Significantly older than controls.
- **Maternal age control group**: >40 years: 3.7% (n=1,028); 30–39 years: 53.6% (n=15,019); 20–29 years: 38.9% (n=10,920); <20 years: 3.8% (n=1,056)
- **BMI RM group**: BMI ≥30 (obese): 21.9% (n=429) vs 14.6% controls (p < 0.05); BMI <18: 0.7% vs 1.2%
- **BMI control group**: BMI ≥30: 14.6% (n=4,085)
- **Parity**: Nulliparous (primary RM): 382/2,030 (18.8%); parous (secondary RM): 1,648/2,030 (81.2%). Control group: nulliparous 11,988/28,023 (42.8%). RM women significantly less likely to be nulliparous (OR 0.31, 95% CI 0.28–0.35).
- **ART/IVF rate**: Assisted conception: 85/2,030 (4.2%) RM group vs 653/28,023 (2.3%) controls (OR 1.83, 95% CI 1.45–2.31)
- **Smoking**: Current smoker: 348/2,030 (17.1%) RM group vs 4,494/28,023 (16.0%) controls (OR 1.08, 95% CI 0.96–1.22; not significant)

## Group C: Exposure & Control Definition
- **RM threshold**: ≥3 miscarriages ("three or more previous miscarriages"); note: not necessarily consecutive
- **Type of losses included**: Three or more previous miscarriages regardless of whether consecutive and regardless of partner; not sub-classified by aetiology (although the hospital has a dedicated RM clinic). Women with two prior miscarriages were included in the comparison group. The paper notes: "the strict definition of recurrent miscarriage is three consecutive miscarriages and while each woman in the study group had three or more miscarriages, they were only confirmed to be consecutive in the primary RM group."
- **Gestational age cutoff for "miscarriage"**: Not explicitly stated but standard definition implied; WHO definition cited as "loss of a pregnancy before viability is achieved." Viability at the hospital defined at 23 weeks gestation (records available for all deliveries >23 weeks).
- **Dose-response analysis**: No — single threshold (≥3 mc) vs. comparison group; subgroup analysis by primary RM vs. secondary RM
- **Control group definition**: All women without a history of three or more miscarriages — includes women with 0, 1, or 2 prior miscarriages (n = 28,023); drawn from the same hospital cohort during the same period. Note: women with 2 miscarriages included in comparison group, which authors note may underestimate the true association.

## Group D: Outcomes & Methods
- **All outcomes measured**:
  - Gestational age at delivery (mean, weeks)
  - Birthweight (mean, grams)
  - Preterm birth (<37 weeks, live birth)
  - Very preterm birth (<32 weeks, live birth)
  - Birthweight <10th centile (SGA using individualized birthweight ratios: http://www.gestation.net — corrected for maternal height and weight, parity, infant sex, ethnicity, gestation)
  - Male infant
  - Apgar score <7 at 5 minutes
  - Admission to neonatal unit
  - Congenital abnormality (identified from physical examination and neonatal discharge records)
  - Aneuploidy (trisomy 15, 18, 21, Turner's syndrome XO)
  - Perinatal death: stillbirth (delivery without signs of life ≥24 weeks or ≥500 g) + early neonatal death (death within first 7 days of life)
  - Pre-eclampsia/pregnancy induced hypertension (PIH): antenatal admission for pre-eclampsia or PIH
  - Abruption/APH: all cases of placental abruption and antepartum haemorrhage requiring delivery
  - Placenta praevia
  - Scheduled (elective) caesarean section
  - Emergency caesarean section
  - Spontaneous vaginal delivery
  - Operative vaginal delivery
  - Spontaneous preterm birth (<37 weeks)
  - Proportion of PTB that were spontaneous
- **Effect measure**: OR (odds ratio) for categorical outcomes; difference in means (95% CI) for continuous outcomes; adjusted OR (adjOR) from multivariable logistic regression
- **Statistical method**: SPSS version 15; univariable logistic regression for baseline characteristics and perinatal outcomes; multivariable stepwise logistic regression for adjusted outcomes; results as proportions, ORs, and 95% CIs
- **Confounders adjusted**: Maternal age (continuous), BMI (continuous, ≥30 classified as obese), marital status (categorical), socio-economic group (categorical), parity (binary), smoking (binary), medical disorders (binary), psychiatric disorders (binary), assisted conception (binary), previous perinatal death (binary). Note: nationality also recorded but not listed as an explicit confounder in the adjustment variables for the main perinatal outcomes table.

## Group E: Results

### Preterm Birth (<37 Weeks)
- **RM group**: 165/2,030 (8.1%)
- **Control group**: 1,551/28,023 (5.5%)
- **Crude OR/RR (95% CI)**: 1.51 (1.28–1.79)
- **Adjusted aOR (95% CI)**: 1.54 (1.29–1.84)
- **p-value**: * (p < 0.05; significant)
- **Subgroup/notes**: Proportion spontaneous PTB: 79/165 (48%) RM group vs 760/1,553 (49%) controls — similar proportions. Primary vs secondary RM: primary 8.6% vs secondary 8.0%; adjOR 1.21 (0.79–1.87) — not significantly different between primary and secondary.

### Very Preterm Birth (<32 Weeks)
- **RM group**: 44/2,030 (2.2%)
- **Control group**: 331/28,023 (1.2%)
- **Crude OR/RR (95% CI)**: 1.85 (1.35–2.55)
- **Adjusted aOR (95% CI)**: 1.80 (1.28–2.53)
- **p-value**: * (p < 0.05; significant)
- **Subgroup/notes**: Primary RM: 3.1% vs secondary RM: 1.9%; adjOR 1.75 (0.83–3.69) — not significant difference between primary and secondary

### Birthweight <10th Centile (SGA, Individualized)
- **RM group**: 263/2,030 (13.0%)
- **Control group**: 3,481/28,023 (12.4%)
- **Crude OR/RR (95% CI)**: 1.05 (0.92–1.20)
- **Adjusted aOR (95% CI)**: 1.07 (0.93–1.23)
- **p-value**: NR (not significant)
- **Subgroup/notes**: No significant association between RM and SGA using individualized birthweight ratios. Primary RM: 11.0% vs secondary RM: 13.4%; adjOR 0.82 (0.57–1.19) — not significant.

### Perinatal Death
- **RM group**: 25/2,030 (1.2%)
- **Control group**: 154/28,023 (0.5%)
- **Crude OR/RR (95% CI)**: 2.26 (1.48–3.45)
- **Adjusted aOR (95% CI)**: 2.66 (1.70–4.14)
- **p-value**: * (p < 0.05; significant; adjusted OR higher than crude)
- **Subgroup/notes**: Primary RM: 1.3% vs secondary RM: 1.2%; adjOR 1.15 (0.41–3.28) — not significantly different between primary and secondary. The adjusted OR exceeds the crude OR, suggesting that confounders (medical disorders, age, BMI) are associated with lower perinatal death risk in the RM group, amplifying the true association.

### Apgar Score <7 at 5 Minutes
- **RM group**: 26/2,030 (1.3%)
- **Control group**: 215/28,023 (0.8%)
- **Crude OR/RR (95% CI)**: 1.68 (1.12–2.53)
- **Adjusted aOR (95% CI)**: 1.54 (0.98–2.42)
- **p-value**: * crude (p < 0.05); adjusted borderline (CI crosses 1.0)
- **Subgroup/notes**: Significant at crude level; loses statistical significance after adjustment

### Admission to Neonatal Unit
- **RM group**: 307/2,030 (15.1%)
- **Control group**: 3,940/28,023 (14.1%)
- **Crude OR/RR (95% CI)**: 1.09 (0.96–1.24)
- **Adjusted aOR (95% CI)**: 1.13 (0.99–1.30)
- **p-value**: NR (not significant overall)
- **Subgroup/notes**: Primary RM: 20.7% vs secondary RM: 13.8%; adjOR 1.76 (1.29–2.39) — significant difference between primary and secondary RM; primarily reflects nulliparity in primary RM group

### Congenital Abnormality

- **Crude OR/RR (95% CI)**: 0.95 (0.55–1.63)

### Pre-eclampsia/PIH

## Group F: Quality Notes
- **Data source quality**: High — large, complete electronic dataset from a single university maternity hospital with very little missing data; accurate gestational age dating; routine ascertainment of perinatal outcomes; neonatal records available for all babies until discharge; 30,053 total participants
- **Confounder adjustment**: Yes — multivariable stepwise logistic regression adjusting for maternal age, BMI, marital status, socio-economic group, parity, smoking, medical disorders, psychiatric disorders, assisted conception, and previous perinatal death. More complete adjustment than most studies in the collection.
- **Outcome validation**: Electronic records with clinical definitions; SGA uses individualized birthweight ratios (gestation.net) corrected for multiple maternal and infant characteristics — a more sophisticated SGA definition than most other studies. Perinatal death definition: stillbirth (≥24 weeks) + early neonatal death (within 7 days; note this is more restrictive than the 28-day neonatal death used in some other studies).
- **Bias acknowledged by authors**: Yes — retrospective design with residual confounding possible; miscarriages not all confirmed consecutive (except primary RM group); women not categorized by aetiology; women with 2 miscarriages in the control group may attenuate the true association; potential recall errors for order of prior pregnancies
- **Other notes**: This is the largest Irish hospital-based cohort study in the collection. Women with 2 miscarriages are in the comparison group, meaning this study compares ≥3 mc vs. ≤2 mc rather than ≥3 mc vs. 0 mc — this likely underestimates the true effect size. The SGA definition (individualized, <10th centile) differs from most other studies (which use <2 SD or <10th centile without individualization) — this may explain why SGA is not significant here while it is in other studies. The adjusted perinatal death OR of 2.66 is notably higher than the crude OR of 2.26, which is unusual and discussed in the notes above. TC Li is explicitly cited in references (Jivraj 2001) as prior work, and Murphy and Field note the same RM definition (≥3 mc).
