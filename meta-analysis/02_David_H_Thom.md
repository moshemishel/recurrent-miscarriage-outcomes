# Thom, 1992 — Meta-Analysis Extraction

## Group A: Study Characteristics
- **Authors**: David H. Thom, Lorene M. Nelson, Thomas L. Vaughan
- **Year**: 1992
- **Journal**: American Journal of Obstetrics and Gynecology (Am J Obstet Gynecol 1992;166:111–6)
- **Country**: United States (Washington State)
- **Data source**: Washington State birth certificate records, 1984–1987. Obstetric ward clerks abstracted information from medical charts into birth certificate forms. Checklist-based format; reporting completeness 80%+ for most birth complications; congenital malformations underreported (32%–57% of hospital discharge diagnoses).
- **Study design**: Retrospective cohort study (population-based, using birth registry data)
- **Study period**: 1984–1987 (birth certificates); index birth year 1987 for one prior SAB and comparison groups; 1984–1987 for ≥3 prior SAB group

## Group B: Population
- **Total N**: 5883 (3099 + 2146 + 638); all singleton live births
- **N RM group**: 638 (women with ≥3 prior spontaneous abortions and no other prior pregnancies)
- **N control group**: 3099 (primigravid women — no prior pregnancies — randomly selected from 1987 birth records)
- **Inclusion criteria**: RM group: all women having a live singleton birth in 1984–1987 with ≥3 prior spontaneous abortions and no other prior pregnancies. Control group: randomly selected women having a live singleton birth in 1987 with no pregnancies prior to the index pregnancy (primigravid). One-SAB group (n=2146): all women having a live singleton birth in 1987 with one prior SAB and no other prior pregnancies. Births ending in fetal death not included (< 0.7% of all pregnancies > 20 weeks).
- **Exclusion criteria**: Fetal deaths (small numbers). Women with prior pregnancies other than spontaneous abortions were excluded from the exposed groups.
- **Maternal age RM group**: Mean 26.3 years; <20 yr 10.3%; 20–24 yr 30.6%; 25–29 yr 30.6%; >30 yr 28.5%
- **Maternal age control group**: Mean 23.9 years; <20 yr 22.8%; 20–24 yr 34.8%; 25–29 yr 26.3%; >30 yr 16.0%
- **BMI RM group**: NR
- **BMI control group**: NR
- **Parity**: By design, all groups were primigravid in terms of prior live births (control group strictly primigravid; exposed groups had only prior SABs, no other pregnancies). This controlled for parity confounding.
- **ART/IVF rate**: NR
- **Smoking**: RM group (≥3 SABs) 36.5%; one-SAB group 28.1%; control (no prior pregnancies) 19.7%; differences highly significant (p < 0.001) after controlling for age

## Group C: Exposure & Control Definition
- **RM threshold**: ≥3 prior spontaneous abortions (primary comparison of interest for RM); also one prior SAB group reported separately
- **Type of losses included**: Spontaneous abortion as defined on Washington State birth certificate (fetal loss before 20 weeks' gestation; first- and second-trimester SABs not differentiated)
- **Gestational age cutoff for "miscarriage"**: Before 20 weeks' gestation (birth certificate definition)
- **Dose-response analysis**: Yes — three groups reported: 0 SABs (control, n=3099), 1 SAB (n=2146), ≥3 SABs (n=638). Separate adjusted RRs presented for each exposure group in Table III. For preterm birth, dose-response within ≥3 SAB group by severity: 34–36 wk RR=1.2 (0.8–1.8); 30–33 wk RR=2.4 (1.4–4.3); <30 wk RR=2.7 (1.0–7.3).
- **Control group definition**: 3099 randomly selected primigravid women having a live singleton birth in 1987 with no pregnancies prior to the index pregnancy; selected to control for parity confounding

## Group D: Outcomes & Methods
- **All outcomes measured**: Low birth weight (<2500 g); intrauterine growth retardation (IUGR; birth weight <10th percentile for gestational age per Brenner nomogram); preterm birth (<37 weeks' gestational age by last menstrual period); gestational age subgroups (34–36 wk, 30–33 wk, <30 wk); congenital malformations (any, excluding ductus arteriosus); placenta previa; prolonged rupture of membranes (>24 hours); breech presentation; 1-minute Apgar score <6; 5-minute Apgar score <7; preeclampsia; abruptio placentae
- **Effect measure**: Relative risk (RR) — described as "relative risk" throughout the paper; estimated via logistic regression (maximum likelihood logistic regression interpreted as RR approximation)
- **Statistical method**: Maximum likelihood logistic regression (Breslow & Day method) to estimate relative risks while controlling for confounding variables
- **Confounders adjusted**: Maternal age and smoking status (adjusted for low birth weight, IUGR, gestational age, preeclampsia); maternal age and gestational length (adjusted for placenta previa, prolonged rupture of membranes, breech presentation); maternal age, smoking status, and gestational length (adjusted for Apgar scores). Variables evaluated but not retained: race, marital status, urban residence, prenatal care, socioeconomic status (mother's household income did not confound in pilot analysis; race, marital status, first-trimester prenatal care did not affect associations).

- **p-value**: NR
- **Subgroup/notes**: One-SAB group: RR = 1.2 (0.9–1.6)

## Group F: Quality Notes
- **Data source quality**: Large population-based birth certificate registry (Washington State). Advantage: large N, population-based, standardized data collection. Limitation: birth certificates known to underreport complications (congenital malformations 32–57% captured; most other complications ≥80%). SAB history taken from medical record before birth outcome recorded, reducing differential misclassification.
- **Confounder adjustment**: Partial — adjusted for maternal age and smoking (primary confounders). Race, marital status, urban residence, prenatal care found not to confound associations and were not retained. Socioeconomic status (household income) did not confound in pilot analysis. BMI not available. Gestational age used as additional confounder for selected outcomes.
- **Outcome validation**: Birth certificate checklist format; completeness 80%+ for most outcomes (preeclampsia, breech, cesarean section); congenital malformations substantially underreported (32–57%). No ICD codes — direct birth certificate fields.
- **Bias acknowledged by authors**: Yes — (1) misclassification of number of prior SABs from birth certificates (non-differential, so would bias toward null); (2) possible association with unmeasured variables (nutrition, alcohol/drug abuse); (3) birth certificates do not distinguish first- vs. second-trimester SABs (potential for second-trimester SAB confounding, particularly in ≥3 SAB group); (4) small numbers limit subgroup analyses.
- **Other notes**: Study design is a key strength: all exposed women had ONLY prior SABs and no other prior pregnancies, controlling for parity confounding. The control group is strictly primigravid. This is one of the earlier large-scale studies; crude rates are presented in Table II; all adjusted estimates are in Table III. Primary comparison of interest for this meta-analysis is ≥3 SABs vs. 0 SABs.
