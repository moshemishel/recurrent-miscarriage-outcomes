# Sheiner, 2005 — Meta-Analysis Extraction

## Group A: Study Characteristics
- **Authors**: Eyal Sheiner, Amalia Levy, Miriam Katz, Moshe Mazor
- **Year**: 2005 (received August 13, 2003; revised March 10, 2004; accepted June 13, 2004; published in European Journal of Obstetrics & Gynecology and Reproductive Biology 118 (2005) 61–65)
- **Journal**: European Journal of Obstetrics & Gynecology and Reproductive Biology
- **Country**: Israel (Beer-Sheva, southern Israel / Negev region)
- **Data source**: Perinatal database at Soroka University Medical Center — information recorded directly after delivery by obstetricians; skilled secretaries examine information before entry; coding done from medical prenatal care records and routine hospital documents. Thrombophilia data (ICD-9 code 286.3) available only for 2000–2002 subperiod. Soroka University Medical Center is the only hospital in the Negev, serving the entire obstetrical population of the region.
- **Study design**: Retrospective cohort study (population-based)
- **Study period**: 1988–2002

## Group B: Population
- **Total N**: 154,294 singleton deliveries
- **N RM group**: 7,503 (4.9% of all deliveries)
- **N control group**: 146,791 (no recurrent abortions)
- **Inclusion criteria**: All singleton pregnancies; deliveries at Soroka University Medical Center 1988–2002. Exposed group: women with ≥2 previous consecutive spontaneous abortions (per ACOG Practice Bulletin 2001 definition).
- **Exclusion criteria**: Not explicitly stated; multiple pregnancies not included (singleton analysis)
- **Maternal age RM group**: 30.1 ± 6 years (mean ± SD)
- **Maternal age control group**: 28.3 ± 6 years (mean ± SD); p < 0.001
- **BMI RM group**: NR
- **BMI control group**: NR
- **Parity**: RM group: gravidity 2–4: 53.4%, gravidity 5+: 46.6% (gravidity 1: 0% by definition); parity 1: 17.9%, parity 2–4: 49.4%, parity 5+: 32.7%. Control group: gravidity 1: 20.2%, gravidity 2–4: 47.9%, gravidity 5+: 31.9%; parity 1: 24.0%, parity 2–4: 50.9%, parity 5+: 29.1%; p < 0.001 for both gravidity and parity distributions.
- **ART/IVF rate**: RM group 2.0% vs. control group 1.6%; OR = 1.2 (1.1–1.4); p = 0.03
- **Smoking**: NR

## Group C: Exposure & Control Definition
- **RM threshold**: ≥2 previous consecutive spontaneous abortions (per ACOG Practice Bulletin 2001 definition; "two or more previous consecutive spontaneous abortions")
- **Type of losses included**: Consecutive spontaneous abortions; further subgrouped as early (≤12 weeks) vs. late (>12 weeks) abortions (early n=7,198; late n=305)
- **Gestational age cutoff for "miscarriage"**: Not explicitly stated as a fixed gestational age for "miscarriage" definition; abortions subgrouped at ≤12 weeks vs. >12 weeks; the broader term "spontaneous abortion" used throughout
- **Dose-response analysis**: No formal dose-response by number of losses. Subgroup analysis by timing of losses: early (≤12 weeks, n=7,198) vs. late (>12 weeks, n=305) abortions (Table 5). Late abortions associated with higher rates of CS and packed-cell transfusion but not Apgar scores or perinatal mortality differences.
- **Control group definition**: All women without a history of recurrent abortions who delivered at Soroka University Medical Center 1988–2002; the entire non-exposed obstetric population of the region

## Group D: Outcomes & Methods
- **All outcomes measured — Obstetric risk factors (Table 2)**: Previous CS; fertility treatments (ovulation induction or IVF); suspected IUGR; mild preeclampsia; severe preeclampsia; chronic hypertension; gestational diabetes mellitus (class A); pre-gestational diabetes mellitus (class B–R); cervical incompetence; PROM (premature rupture of membranes); placenta previa. **Labor/delivery complications (Table 3)**: Abruptio placenta; meconium-stained amniotic fluid; mal-presentations; cesarean delivery; Apgar 1 min <7; Apgar 5 min <7; perinatal mortality; post-partum hemorrhage; blood products transfusion. **Thrombophilia subanalysis (Table 6)**: IUGR, abruptio placenta, CS, Apgar <7 (1 min, 5 min), perinatal mortality, post-partum hemorrhage, among recurrent abortion cases with vs. without thrombophilia (2000–2002 only).
- **Effect measure**: OR (odds ratio) for crude associations (Tables 2, 3); adjusted OR from multivariate logistic regression (Table 4 for independent risk factors associated with recurrent abortions; Table 7 for CS specifically)
- **Statistical method**: Chi-square test or Fisher's exact test (categorical variables); t-test (continuous variables); multivariate logistic regression model with backward elimination (to identify independent risk factors associated with recurrent abortions); Mantel–Haenszel technique (stratified analysis for CS association); SPSS statistical package
- **Confounders adjusted**: Table 4 (multivariate logistic regression for factors independently associated with recurrent abortions — this is a model to identify characteristics of RM women, not to adjust for confounders in outcome analysis per se). Table 7 (CS risk): Mantel–Haenszel adjusted for placenta previa, abruptio placenta, diabetes mellitus, hypertensive disorders, previous CS, mal-presentations, PROM, fertility treatments. Additional multivariate model for CS controlling for same variables confirmed recurrent abortion as independent predictor.

## Group F: Quality Notes
- **Data source quality**: Large population-based database from single-center serving entire Negev region (no other hospitals in region, so population is non-discriminating/complete). Perinatal database with quality controls (skilled secretaries, coding from medical records). Long study period (1988–2002) captures large numbers. Limitation: thrombophilia data only available 2000–2002.
- **Confounder adjustment**: Partial. Crude ORs presented for most outcomes (Tables 2, 3). A multivariate logistic regression identifies characteristics independently associated with recurrent abortion status (Table 4) — this is essentially identifying correlates of RM, not adjusting outcomes for confounders. For CS specifically, Mantel–Haenszel and separate multivariate analysis confirms independent association. No comprehensive multivariable adjustment for all outcomes. Age, parity, fertility treatments, previous CS, and multiple comorbidities are all confounders that differed between groups and were not formally adjusted for most outcomes.
- **Outcome validation**: Perinatal database with ICD-9 code 286.3 for thrombophilia; other outcomes from clinical records entered after delivery by obstetricians. Coding done from medical records.
- **Bias acknowledged by authors**: Yes — (1) higher incidence of RM (4.9%) than literature (0.5–3%) attributed to using ≥2 consecutive losses (vs. ≥3 in some literature); (2) possible caregiver bias toward CS in high-risk patients (history of infertility or recurrent abortions); (3) most patients did not undergo thrombophilia screening (study started 1988 before thrombophilia evidence was in common practice); (4) small number of thrombophilia cases (n=22 in RM group 2000–2002) precludes firm conclusions.
- **Other notes**: The paper's multivariate analysis (Table 4) identifies which conditions are independently associated with being in the RM group — it is not a standard outcome regression. The crude ORs in Tables 2 and 3 are the primary data for most outcomes. Key findings: placenta previa (crude OR 2.2, adjusted 1.7), abruptio placenta (crude OR 1.6, adjusted 1.5), hypertensive disorders (adjusted OR 1.2), diabetes (adjusted OR 1.7), cervical incompetence (adjusted OR 12.0), and CS (adjusted OR 1.2) all independently associated with recurrent abortion. Notably, suspected IUGR, mild preeclampsia, Apgar scores, and perinatal mortality were NOT significantly associated.
