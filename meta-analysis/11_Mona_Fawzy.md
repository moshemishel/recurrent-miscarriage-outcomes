# Fawzy, 2016 — Meta-Analysis Extraction

## Group A: Study Characteristics
- **Authors**: Mona Fawzy, Sotirios Saravelos, Tin Chiu Li, Mostafa Metwally
- **Year**: 2016
- **Journal**: Human Fertility (Vol. 19, No. 1, pp. 9–15)
- **Country**: United Kingdom (Sheffield, England)
- **Data source**: Two hospital databases: (1) recurrent miscarriage database at the Jessop Wing, Royal Hallamshire Hospital (prospectively collected data from RM clinic patients); (2) general obstetric database (Protos evolution) at the same hospital, including all births at hospital and at home
- **Study design**: Retrospective case-control study (analysis of prospectively collected data)
- **Study period**: January 2001 – July 2007

## Group B: Population
- **Total N**: 40,260 (400 RM patients + 39,860 controls)
- **N RM group**: 400 consecutive women with history of recurrent miscarriage who achieved pregnancies progressing beyond 24 weeks gestation
- **N control group**: 39,860 deliveries from the general obstetric database during the same period
- **Inclusion criteria**: RM group: consecutive women who attended the recurrent miscarriage clinic at the Jessop Wing and achieved pregnancies progressing beyond 24 weeks gestation between January 2001 and July 2007. Control group: all births in the hospital database during the same period (including home births); babies admitted to SCBU from other units and twins were excluded.
- **Exclusion criteria**: Twins and babies admitted to SCBU from other units excluded from both groups
- **Maternal age RM group**: 32.6 ± 5.7 years (mean ± SD); secondary RM patients significantly older than primary (34.0 ± 5.4 vs 31.7 ± 5.7 years)
- **Maternal age control group**: 29.0 ± 6.2 years (mean ± SD); significantly younger than study group (p < 0.0001)
- **BMI RM group**: Median 24.1 (range 16.1–49.9) kg/m²; primary RM subgroup median 23.6 (19–44) kg/m²; secondary RM subgroup median 29.1 (23–33) kg/m²
- **BMI control group**: NR
- **Parity**: 143 (36%) had primary miscarriage (no prior live birth); 257 (64%) had secondary miscarriage (at least one prior live birth)
- **ART/IVF rate**: NR
- **Smoking**: NR

## Group C: Exposure & Control Definition
- **RM threshold**: ≥3 consecutive spontaneous miscarriages before 20 weeks gestation (three consecutive spontaneous miscarriages)
- **Type of losses included**: Three or more consecutive spontaneous miscarriages; antiphospholipid syndrome cases treated with enoxaparin and aspirin included; uterine anomalies treated surgically included; full investigative protocol required
- **Gestational age cutoff for "miscarriage"**: Before 20 weeks gestation
- **Dose-response analysis**: No — single threshold (≥3 mc) vs. control; subgroup analyses by type (primary vs. secondary RM, unexplained RM, fetal sex)
- **Control group definition**: All deliveries from the general obstetric database (Protos evolution) at the Jessop Wing, Royal Hallamshire Hospital during the same period (January 2001 – July 2007), n = 39,860; includes all births at hospital and at home; babies from other units transferred to SCBU and twins excluded

## Group D: Outcomes & Methods
- **All outcomes measured**:
  - Antepartum haemorrhage (APH): vaginal bleeding from 24 weeks prior to birth
  - Gestational diabetes (WHO criteria: fasting glucose >9 mmol/L or 2-hour >11 mmol/L after 75 g oral glucose)
  - Pregnancy induced hypertension (PIH): diastolic BP >90 mmHg on two occasions ≥4 hours apart, first diagnosed after 20 weeks
  - Preterm labour (PTL): babies born before 37 completed weeks gestation
  - Emergency caesarean section rate
  - Foetal sex ratio (male/female)
  - Intrapartum foetal distress: pathological heart trace pattern during continuous foetal monitoring
  - Small for gestational age (SGA): foetal growth <2 SD below mean for gestational age (growth and development record charts)
  - Admission to special care baby unit (SCBU)
  - Stillbirth: babies born after 24 completed weeks without signs of life
  - Neonatal death: death within 28 days of birth
  - Perinatal death: combination of all stillbirths and neonatal deaths
  - Apgar score <6 at 1 minute
  - Apgar score <6 at 5 minutes
- **Effect measure**: OR (odds ratio) with 95% CI; Mantel-Haenszel fixed-effect method (RevMan software)
- **Statistical method**: Chi-squared test or Mann-Whitney test for group comparisons; OR and 95% CI calculated using RevMan software (Mantel-Haenszel fixed-effect); no multivariable regression for primary analysis — ORs are unadjusted
- **Confounders adjusted**: None adjusted in primary analysis (crude ORs only); subgroup analyses used to address some confounders (primary vs. secondary RM; unexplained RM group; sex of fetus)

## Group E: Results

### Antepartum Haemorrhage (APH)
- **RM group**: 12/400, 3.0%
- **Control group**: 160/39,860, 0.4%
- **Crude OR/RR (95% CI)**: 7.67 (4.23–13.91)
- **Adjusted aOR (95% CI)**: NR (crude only)
- **p-value**: NR (significant)
- **Subgroup/notes**: Unexplained RM subgroup: OR 6.71 (2.45–18.32), significant. This was the only maternal outcome that was significantly increased.

### Gestational Diabetes
- **RM group**: 8/400, 2.0%
- **Control group**: 485/29,860 (note: denominator differs — 29,860 in figure data), 1.6%
- **Crude OR/RR (95% CI)**: 1.24 (0.61–2.50)
- **Adjusted aOR (95% CI)**: NR
- **p-value**: NR (not significant)
- **Subgroup/notes**: Unexplained RM: OR 0.40 (0.06–2.87), not significant

### Pregnancy Induced Hypertension (PIH)

- **Subgroup/notes**: Unexplained RM: OR 1.25 (0.68–2.31), not significant. Primary RM vs. primiparous controls: OR 3.79 (1.88–7.63), significant — increased PIH risk when comparing primary RM women to primiparous-only controls.

## Group F: Quality Notes
- **Data source quality**: Moderate — RM group data collected prospectively as part of clinic care; control data from hospital obstetric database. Both from the same institution. RM group underwent standardized investigation protocol; control group did not. Hospital is a large tertiary referral centre, which may skew the control population toward higher-risk pregnancies (authors acknowledge this but note it would minimize, not accentuate, differences).
- **Confounder adjustment**: No — primary analysis uses only crude ORs (Mantel-Haenszel); subgroup analyses (primary RM vs. primiparous controls; unexplained RM) partially address parity and aetiology confounding. No multivariable regression performed. Age not adjusted for (RM group significantly older than controls, p < 0.0001).
- **Outcome validation**: Hospital database records; clinical definitions for each outcome are specified; no ICD coding mentioned
- **Bias acknowledged by authors**: Yes — tertiary referral centre may skew RM group toward more complex pathologies; ethnicity data not available; higher proportion of secondary vs. primary RM patients (explained by greater chance of success in secondary RM); age difference between groups not adjusted. Authors note this as an important limitation.
- **Other notes**: This is a follow-on study from Jivraj et al. 2001 (same centre, same RM definition); the current study uses a substantially larger RM cohort (400 vs. 162). Subgroup analysis showing male fetus association with worse outcomes is a unique finding in this collection. No adjustment for age, BMI, or parity in the primary analysis is a significant limitation. The study uses a ≥3 consecutive miscarriages threshold, consistent with the older UK definition of RM.
