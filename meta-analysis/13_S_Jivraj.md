# Jivraj, 2001 — Meta-Analysis Extraction

## Group A: Study Characteristics
- **Authors**: S. Jivraj, B. Anstie, Y-C. Cheong, F.M. Fairlie, S.M. Laird, T.C. Li
- **Year**: 2001
- **Journal**: Human Reproduction (Volume 16, Issue 1, January 2001, Pages 102–106)
- **Country**: United Kingdom (Sheffield, England)
- **Data source**: (1) RM group: hospital records of patients attending the recurrent miscarriage clinic at the Jessop Hospital for Women, Sheffield, identified via hospital computer system and case note retrieval; (2) Control group: hospital database of all deliveries at Jessop Hospital for Women during the same period
- **Study design**: Retrospective cohort study
- **Study period**: 1 January 1992 – 30 June 1998

## Group B: Population
- **Total N**: 162 + 24,699 = 24,861 (RM group + control)
- **N RM group**: 162 pregnancies (from 178 eligible women who had ≥1 singleton pregnancy progressing beyond 24 weeks; only first singleton delivery retrieved; 91% case note retrieval rate)
- **N control group**: 24,699 singleton deliveries (multiple pregnancies excluded)
- **Inclusion criteria**: RM group: patients with recurrent (≥3) miscarriage attending the clinic between 1 January 1992 and 30 June 1998; had at least one singleton pregnancy progressing beyond 24 weeks and delivering during the study period; only the first such pregnancy included. Control group: all deliveries from singleton pregnancies at the Jessop Hospital for Women during the same period.
- **Exclusion criteria**: RM group: multiple pregnancies excluded; 16 patients whose case notes were retrieved but delivered at surrounding district general hospitals excluded (to maintain consistency with local control population). Control group: multiple pregnancies excluded.
- **Maternal age RM group**: Mean ± SD: 32.0 ± 5.4 years at conception; mean number of miscarriages 3.4 (range 3–10)
- **Maternal age control group**: Mean ± SD: 28.3 ± 5.4 years
- **BMI RM group**: NR
- **BMI control group**: NR
- **Parity**: RM group: 95 (59%) primary miscarriage (no prior live birth); 67 (41%) secondary miscarriage (≥1 prior live birth); mean parity 0.6 ± 0.8. Control group: mean parity 0.8 ± 1.1.
- **ART/IVF rate**: NR
- **Smoking**: NR

## Group C: Exposure & Control Definition
- **RM threshold**: ≥3 consecutive spontaneous miscarriages ("recurrent (≥3) miscarriages")
- **Type of losses included**: Three or more consecutive spontaneous miscarriages; etiological subgroups included: unknown (28%), unexplained (38%), prothrombotic state (13.5%), structural uterine abnormality (7%), endocrine factors (13.5%), chromosomal anomalies (0%). All women underwent a comprehensive investigative protocol; those with identifiable causes received appropriate treatment.
- **Gestational age cutoff for "miscarriage"**: Not explicitly stated; the standard definition implies first-trimester/early pregnancy losses; viability defined at 24 weeks (study restricted to deliveries beyond 24 weeks)
- **Dose-response analysis**: No — single threshold (≥3 mc) vs. control; subgroup analyses by primary vs. secondary miscarriage, and unexplained miscarriage
- **Control group definition**: All singleton deliveries from the Jessop Hospital for Women database between 1 January 1992 and 30 June 1998 (n = 24,699); controls are the general obstetric population of the same hospital

## Group D: Outcomes & Methods
- **All outcomes measured**:
  - Hypertension: overall (chronic hypertension + pregnancy-induced hypertension/pre-eclampsia)
  - Chronic hypertension: diastolic BP >90 mmHg (pre-existing)
  - Pregnancy-induced hypertension (PIH) or pre-eclampsia: diastolic BP >90 mmHg on two occasions ≥4 hours apart, first diagnosed after relevant gestation
  - Diabetes: fasting venous plasma glucose >9 mmol/L or 2-hour >11 mmol/L after 75 g oral glucose (WHO criteria)
  - Preterm delivery (<37 completed weeks gestation)
  - Small for gestational age (SGA): birthweight <2 SD below mean for gestational age (Keen and Pearse, 1988 growth charts)
  - Caesarean section: overall, emergency, elective
  - Vaginal delivery: normal, instrumental
  - Perinatal mortality rate: stillbirths (born after 24 completed weeks without signs of life) + neonatal deaths (death within 28 days of birth), expressed as percentage of all deliveries
  - Admission to special care baby unit (SCBU)
- **Effect measure**: Proportions/rates compared; no OR or RR reported in primary analysis — chi-squared test used
- **Statistical method**: Chi-squared test for comparisons between study and control group
- **Confounders adjusted**: None — no multivariable regression performed; authors acknowledge age and parity differences between groups but argue bias is unlikely to be significant given small differences. Parity addressed via subgroup (primary vs. secondary RM) but no statistical adjustment.

## Group E: Results

### Preterm Delivery (<37 Weeks)
- **RM group (whole, n=162)**: 22/162, 13.6% (reported as 13% in text and 13.3% in results; table shows 22 [13%])
- **Control group (n=24,699)**: 959/24,699, 3.9%
- **Crude OR/RR (95% CI)**: NR (chi-squared used; p < 0.01)
- **Adjusted aOR (95% CI)**: NR
- **p-value**: p < 0.01
- **Subgroup/notes**: Primary RM: 17/95 (18%); secondary RM: 5/67 (7.7%); unexplained RM: 7/61 (12%). Rate in primary RM more than double that of secondary RM.

### Small for Gestational Age (SGA)
- **RM group (whole, n=162)**: 21/162, 13.0%
- **Control group (n=24,699)**: 523/24,699, 2.1%
- **Crude OR/RR (95% CI)**: NR (chi-squared; p < 0.01)
- **Adjusted aOR (95% CI)**: NR
- **p-value**: p < 0.01
- **Subgroup/notes**: Primary RM: 12/95 (13%); secondary RM: 9/67 (13%); unexplained RM: 5/61 (8.2%). Similar rates across all RM subgroups.

### Caesarean Section (Overall)
- **RM group (whole, n=162)**: 60/162, 36.0%
- **Control group (n=24,699)**: 4,124/24,699, 16.7%

- **Adjusted aOR (95% CI)**: NR
- **p-value**: p < 0.01
- **Subgroup/notes**: Emergency CS: 30/162 (18%) vs 2,519/24,699 (10.2%), p < 0.01. Elective CS: 30/162 (18%) vs 1,605/24,699 (6.5%), p < 0.01. Primary RM: 35/95 (37%); secondary RM: 25/67 (37%); unexplained RM: 24/61 (39%).

## Group F: Quality Notes
- **Data source quality**: Moderate — hospital-based cohort with detailed RM investigation protocol; case note retrieval rate 91%; controls from same hospital database. 16 RM cases (9%) delivered elsewhere and were excluded — authors note this is unlikely to substantially affect results. Control group is the general obstetric population without parity or age matching.
- **Confounder adjustment**: No — no multivariable regression; chi-squared only. Age difference acknowledged (RM: mean 32.0 yrs vs. control: 28.3 yrs) but not adjusted; authors argue bias is small. Parity addressed through subgroup analysis (primary vs. secondary RM) but no statistical matching or adjustment.
- **Outcome validation**: Clinical case note review; standardized clinical definitions provided for all outcomes; SGA defined per Keen and Pearse (1988) charts
- **Bias acknowledged by authors**: Yes — age and parity not matched to controls; 9% case note loss (delivered at district hospitals, likely lower-risk); Caesarean section rate in control group increased over study period (12.4% in 1992 to 22.9% in 1998), which may introduce temporal confounding
- **Other notes**: This is one of the most frequently cited studies in the collection and was the original study from the Jessop Wing Sheffield RM clinic reporting obstetric outcomes. It preceded Fawzy 2016 (same centre, same clinic) by 15 years and used a smaller cohort (162 vs. 400 RM patients) with a shorter control database (24,699 vs. 39,860). Effect measures reported as raw proportions only with chi-squared significance testing — no ORs or RRs calculated. The RM threshold is ≥3 consecutive miscarriages. Results are often reported in ranges in the abstract (preterm 13%, SGA 13%, perinatal loss 2.5%, CS 36%) vs. controls (3.9%, 2.1%, 1%, 16.7%). TC Li is the last/senior author.
