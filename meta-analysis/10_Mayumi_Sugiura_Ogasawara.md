# Sugiura-Ogasawara, 2019 — Meta-Analysis Extraction

## Group A: Study Characteristics
- **Authors**: Mayumi Sugiura-Ogasawara, Takeshi Ebara, Yasuyuki Yamada, Naoto Shoji, Taro Matsuki, Hirohisa Kano, Takahiro Kurihara, Toyonori Omori, Motohiro Tomizawa, Maiko Miyata, Michihiro Kamijima, Shinji Saitoh, and the Japan Environment and Children's Study (JECS) Group
- **Year**: 2019 (received June 2018, accepted October 2018; published in American Journal of Reproductive Immunology 2019;81:e13072)
- **Journal**: American Journal of Reproductive Immunology
- **Country**: Japan
- **Data source**: Japan Environment and Children's Study (JECS) — a nationwide population-based birth cohort study conducted by the Ministry of the Environment, Government of Japan. Data collected via self-administered questionnaires and medical record transcriptions by doctors/research coordinators at multiple time points (registration, just after delivery, 1 month after delivery). Dataset: jecs-ag-20160424 (released July 2016), comprising 104,102 registered pregnancies from 15 regional JECS centers.
- **Study design**: Prospective population-based birth cohort study (recruited prospectively; exposure was a self-reported and doctor-verified historical variable)
- **Study period**: Recruitment January 31, 2011 – March 31, 2014

## Group B: Population
- **Total N**: 96,212 participants in main analysis (after exclusions); histories of previous pregnancy losses available for 94,658
- **N RM group**: 1,065 with ≥3 previous pregnancy losses; 3,633 with 2 previous losses (also analyzed separately as a tier)
- **N control group**: 73,413 with no previous pregnancy loss
- **Inclusion criteria**: Pregnant women recruited by JECS between January 31, 2011 and March 31, 2014; residing in study areas of 15 regional centers; expected delivery date after August 1, 2011; capable of comprehending Japanese and completing self-administered questionnaire
- **Exclusion criteria**: Multiple pregnancies (n = 1,994 children excluded); fetuses/embryos terminated by induced abortion (n = 310); women who participated for the second or third time (n = 5,586 children excluded)
- **Maternal age RM group**: ≥3 losses: >40 years 16.3% (n=167), 30–39 years 67.9% (n=694), 20–29 years 15.5% (n=158), <20 years 0.3% (n=3)
- **Maternal age control group**: 0 losses: >40 years 2.8% (n=1,971), 30–39 years 52.9% (n=37,392), 20–29 years 42.8% (n=30,278), <20 years 1.5% (n=1,026). Mean (SD) age at registration overall: 30.7 (5.1) years
- **BMI RM group**: ≥3 losses: ≥25.0 kg/m²: 12.9% (n=137), 18.5–25.0: 72.7% (n=773), <18.5: 14.5% (n=154)
- **BMI control group**: 0 losses: ≥25.0 kg/m²: 10.4%, 18.5–25.0: 72.9%, <18.5: 16.7%
- **Parity**: Previous live birth: 79.0% (≥3 losses) vs 52.1% (0 losses); mixed parity population
- **ART/IVF rate**: IVF-ET carried out: 9.4% (≥3 losses) vs 2.7% (0 losses); 5.2% (2 losses); 4.2% (1 loss)
- **Smoking**: Current smoker: 6.8% (≥3 losses), 7.2% (2 losses), 5.3% (1 loss) vs 4.5% (0 losses); quit before pregnancy: 29.7% (≥3 losses) vs 22.3% (0 losses)

## Group C: Exposure & Control Definition
- **RM threshold**: Dose-response analysis with four tiers: 0, 1, 2, and 3 or more previous pregnancy losses (PL); no single RM cutoff applied
- **Type of losses included**: All pregnancy losses reported by participants in the self-administered questionnaire (MT1), with priority given to doctor-recorded data from medical record transcription (DrT1); includes miscarriage, stillbirth, induced abortion distinguished in records. "Recurrent pregnancy loss (RPL) is defined as two or more losses at any time during pregnancy"
- **Gestational age cutoff for "miscarriage"**: "Most of these occur before 12 weeks of gestation." RPL defined as losses at any time during pregnancy. Late miscarriage and stillbirth analyzed as a separate outcome (>20 weeks gestation); early miscarriage analyzed separately (<12 weeks gestation)
- **Dose-response analysis**: Yes — four tiers (0, 1, 2, ≥3 previous PL) for all outcomes; OR reported for each tier vs. 0-loss reference
- **Control group definition**: Women with no previous pregnancy loss (n = 73,413), drawn from the same JECS birth cohort

## Group D: Outcomes & Methods
- **All outcomes measured**:
  - Late miscarriage and stillbirth >20 weeks gestation (combined endpoint)
  - Early miscarriage <12 weeks gestation (in current pregnancy)
  - Pregnancy loss (any, combined)
  - Preterm birth <37 weeks gestation
  - Preterm birth <34 weeks gestation
  - Premature rupture of membranes (PROM)
  - Placenta praevia
  - Oligohydramnios
  - Abruptio placentae
  - Placental adhesion
  - Hypertensive disorders of pregnancy — mild (HDP mild)
  - Hypertensive disorders of pregnancy — severe (HDP severe)
  - Uterine infection
  - Caesarean section
  - Small for date (SFD, birthweight <10th percentile)
  - Intrauterine fetal death (IUFD)
  - Low birth weight (<2,500 g)
  - Infant sex (male)
  - Apgar score <7 at 5 minutes
  - Umbilical artery blood pH <7.1
  - Congenital anomaly
  - Aneuploidy
- **Effect measure**: OR (odds ratio) with 95% CI
- **Statistical method**: Logistic regression with propensity score adjustment; four models reported: Model I (unadjusted), Model II (adjusted, complete-case analysis with propensity score), Model III (adjusted with multiple imputation analyses [MIA] and propensity score), Model IV (MIA + gynecological history covariates). Primary results are from Model III.
- **Confounders adjusted**: Varied by outcome (propensity score for each outcome included only covariates with P < .05 in univariable analysis); covariates considered included: maternal age, BMI, marital status, IVF-ET, previous live birth, smoking, education, income, and extensive list of medical/gynecological histories (atopic dermatitis, asthma, collagen disease, autoimmune disease, SLE, RA, IDDM, NIDDM, gestational diabetes, hyperthyroidism, hypothyroidism, anemia, hypertension, hyperlipidemia, stroke, myocardial infarction, congenital heart disease, Kawasaki disease, depression, dysautonomia, anxiety disorder, gastroesophageal reflux disease, gastritis, gastric ulcer, duodenal ulcer, irritable colon, Crohn's disease, ulcerative colitis, fatty liver, chronic nephritis, nephrotic syndrome, menstrual disorder, endometriosis, adenomyosis, uterine fibroids, uterine anomaly, ovarian tumor, PCOS)

## Group E: Results

### Late Miscarriage and Stillbirth >20 Weeks Gestation
- **RM group (≥3 losses)**: 1.6% (17/1,034)
- **Control group (0 losses)**: 0.7% (534/71,708)
- **Crude OR/RR (95% CI)**: 2.23 (1.37–3.63) [Model I]
- **Adjusted aOR (95% CI)**: Model III (MIA): 1.76 (1.04–2.96); Model II: 2.12 (1.23–3.65)
- **p-value**: NR (statistically significant at 0.05 level for ≥3 losses)
- **Subgroup/notes**: Dose-response by tier — 1 loss: aOR Model III 1.18 (0.99–1.42); 2 losses: aOR Model III 1.34 (0.97–1.84). Significant only at ≥3 losses tier in Model III.

## Group F: Quality Notes
- **Data source quality**: High — large-scale nationwide birth cohort (n > 96,000) with prospective data collection, multiple data sources (self-report + medical records), and independent validation via doctor records. JECS is a government-funded study with standardized protocols.
- **Confounder adjustment**: Yes — extensive; outcome-specific propensity scores incorporating up to 30+ covariates; four models with increasing adjustment rigor; MIA used to handle 5.2%–21.1% missing data across variables
- **Outcome validation**: Medical record transcription by doctors; ICD coding not explicitly mentioned but medical records used; congenital anomaly data from physical examination and neonatal discharge records; chromosomal aneuploidy from doctor records given priority
- **Bias acknowledged by authors**: Yes — concerns about generalizability due to missing data addressed via MIA; acknowledged that women with PL could have greater chance of being diagnosed with gynecological disease due to increased surveillance (hence Model IV adding gynecological history covariates); no information on induced abortion in some records
- **Other notes**: The use of multiple imputation analyses (MIA) is a key methodological distinction. Some outcomes became significant only in Model III (MIA), including PTB <37 weeks and uterine infection — illustrating impact of missing data handling. The paper uses the term "pregnancy loss" (PL) rather than "miscarriage" and includes losses at any gestational age. The dose-response design mirrors Gunnarsdottir 2014 but in a Japanese population. The paper reports 2019 as publication year; the YAML/header states DOI 10.1111/aji.13072.
