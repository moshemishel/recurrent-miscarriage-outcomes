# Hautamäki, 2025 — Meta-Analysis Extraction

## Group A: Study Characteristics
- **Authors**: Hanna Hautamäki, Mika Gissler, Jenni Heikkinen-Eloranta, Aila Tiitinen, Pirkko Peuranpää
- **Year**: Published online ahead of print (received September 25, 2024; revised November 15, 2024; accepted December 5, 2024; DOI: 10.1111/aogs.15039; journal: Acta Obstetricia et Gynecologica Scandinavica)
- **Journal**: Acta Obstetricia et Gynecologica Scandinavica
- **Country**: Finland (Helsinki)
- **Data source**: Hospital medical records (clinical data from Helsinki University Hospital RPL referrals) linked with Finnish Medical Birth Register (MBR; managed by Finnish Institute for Health and Welfare; covers all Finnish births since 1987; complete coverage with very low missing data rate; physician-coded ICD-10 diagnoses); data linkage via pseudonymized personal identity numbers through Finnish Social and Health Data Permit Authority Findata
- **Study design**: Retrospective case–control study (cases: RPL women referred to Helsinki University Hospital; controls: matched from MBR)
- **Study period**: RPL examinations 2007–2016; follow-up until December 31, 2019

## Group B: Population
- **Total N**: 1,800 (360 RPL cases + 1,440 controls)
- **N RM group**: 360 women (first singleton birth after RPL investigations; from 506 referred couples, 71.1% had singleton birth)
- **N control group**: 1,440 women (4 controls per case matched from MBR)
- **Inclusion criteria (cases)**: Couples referred to Helsinki University Hospital for RPL between 2007 and 2016; study examines first pregnancy after RPL investigations ending in singleton birth. Most couples studied after ≥3 consecutive clinical first-trimester pregnancy losses OR ≥2 losses with at least one between GWs 12+1 and 21+6 when woman's age was <42 years; all met ESHRE definition for RPL. Only first singleton birth included.
- **Inclusion criteria (controls)**: Four controls selected from MBR for each RPL woman, matched for age, parity, year, month, and hospital of delivery. Control women with RPL (≥3 miscarriages, n=27) excluded from sub-analyses.
- **Exclusion criteria**: Multiple pregnancies (singleton births only); non-first subsequent pregnancy after RPL investigations
- **Maternal age RM group**: Mean 34.4 years (SD 4.85); <25: 4.2%; 25–29: 14.2%; 30–34: 35.8%; 35–39: 33.6%; ≥40: 12.2%
- **Maternal age control group**: Mean 34.4 years (SD 4.84) — matched by design; p = 1.00
- **BMI RM group**: Mean 24.43 kg/m² (SD 4.43) in early pregnancy; <20: 13.1%; 20–24.9: 49.7%; 25–29.9: 22.8%; ≥30: 12.5%; unknown: 1.9%
- **BMI control group**: Mean 24.09 kg/m² (SD 4.65); p = 0.19 (not significantly different)
- **Parity**: Matched by design (primipara n=163 RPL / n=652 controls; multipara n=197 RPL / n=788 controls)
- **ART/IVF rate**: RPL group: 13.1% any assisted reproduction (IVF 3.6%, insemination 1.4%, ovulation induction 8.1%); control group: 9.9% (IVF 1.3%, insemination 2.0%, ovulation induction 6.6%); p = 0.01
- **Smoking**: RPL group: current smoker 4.7%, stopped first trimester 2.2%, not smoking 90.3%; control group: current smoker 5.4%, stopped first trimester 4.2%, not smoking 87.6%; p = 0.34 (not significant)

## Group C: Exposure & Control Definition
- **RM threshold**: ≥2 pregnancy losses (ESHRE definition); in practice, most couples were studied after ≥3 consecutive clinical first-trimester losses OR ≥2 losses with at least one in the second trimester (GWs 12+1 to 21+6) when age <42 years
- **Type of losses included**: Consecutive and non-consecutive; clinical first-trimester losses; also second-trimester losses (12+1 to 21+6 GWs) if at least one such loss present. Both explained and unexplained RPL included.
- **Gestational age cutoff for "miscarriage"**: Before 22+0 gestational weeks (live birth defined as child born alive; stillbirth defined as stillborn ≥22+0 weeks or birthweight ≥500 g)
- **Dose-response analysis**: No formal dose-response by number of losses. Sub-analyses comparing: (1) secondary RPL (n=197) vs. multipara controls (n=788); (2) unexplained RPL (n=228) vs. explained RPL (n=132).
- **Control group definition**: Four women selected from MBR for each RPL woman, matched for age, parity, year and month of delivery, and hospital of delivery. Controls without the matching criteria having RPL (≥3 miscarriages, n=27) excluded from sub-analyses.

## Group D: Outcomes & Methods
- **All outcomes measured**: Primary: pregnancy and delivery complications, perinatal outcomes, characteristics of pregnancy follow-up. Secondary: maternal and neonatal ICD-10 diagnoses. Specific outcomes: gestational hypertension (ICD-10 O13; elevation of previously normal BP >140/90 mmHg after 20 weeks without proteinuria); preeclampsia (ICD-10 O14; newly onset hypertension after 20 GWs plus proteinuria >300 mg/24h); GDM (ICD-10 O24.4; diagnosed by oral glucose tolerance test 75g at 24–28 weeks; adjusted for BMI); preterm birth (<37 GWs); malpresentation (ICD-10 O32); PROM (ICD-10 O42); induced labor; mode of delivery (vaginal, vacuum/forceps, planned cesarean, urgent/emergency cesarean); placental problems composite (ICD-10 O43, O44, O45, or manual removal of placenta); manual removal of placenta; placenta previa (ICD-10 O44); placental abruption (ICD-10 O45); postpartum hemorrhage (ICD-10 O72); neonatal composite (Apgar 5-min 0–6, NICU admission, perinatal death, umbilical artery pH <7.10); prematurity composite (SGA, gestational age <37 weeks, diagnoses P07, O42, O10–O14); birthweight (mean and categories); SGA (birth weight <−2 SDs in Finnish growth curves); umbilical artery pH <7.10; Apgar scores (1 min, 5 min); NICU admission; perinatal death; diagnoses P02 (newborn affected by complications of placenta, cord, membranes); P07 (disorders related to short gestation and low birthweight); preterm labor (ICD-10 O60); cervical incompetence (ICD-10 O34.3); fear of childbirth diagnosis; number of outpatient visits; length of hospital stay.
- **Effect measure**: RR (risk ratio; 95% CI); OR for GDM adjusted with logistic regression for BMI
- **Statistical method**: Chi-squared test for independence (categorical outcomes); t-test or Mann–Whitney U test (continuous); risk ratios with 95% CIs calculated for all outcomes; logistic regression for BMI-adjusted GDM OR. Outcomes with <5 cases per cell not reported due to data protection rules.
- **Confounders adjusted**: BMI adjustment for GDM only (OR 1.33, 95% CI 0.95–1.82 after BMI adjustment, p = 0.10 — became non-significant). All other outcomes: unadjusted RRs. Potential confounders controlled by matching design: age, parity, hospital, time period.

## Group F: Quality Notes
- **Data source quality**: Strong study design with thoroughly investigated and diagnosed RPL couples (clinical data) linked to Finnish MBR (register data). MBR has collected information on all Finnish newborns since 1987; information routinely registered; missing/incorrect data double-checked from birth hospitals; excellent coverage and good validity in published data quality studies. Complete follow-up data on pregnancies and neonates for all 360 RPL women (no loss to follow-up for primary outcomes). Data for cases and controls collected similarly from same national register.
- **Confounder adjustment**: Minimal — only BMI adjustment for GDM (via logistic regression). All other outcomes reported as crude RRs. Confounding controlled primarily through matching (age, parity, hospital, delivery time period). BMI not significantly different between groups (matched controls). Smoking similar between groups. Key unmatched confounders: marital status (more single/divorced in RPL: 4.4% vs. 2.4%), ART use (13.1% vs. 9.9%).
- **Outcome validation**: ICD-10 coded diagnoses from MBR; physician-coded; data quality validated through Finnish MBR data quality studies. Due to strict Finnish data protection rules, outcomes with 1–4 cases per group not reported (several outcomes thus suppressed).
- **Bias acknowledged by authors**: Yes — (1) possible insufficient power for rare outcomes (stillbirth, placental abruption) due to small sample size (n=360 cases); (2) data protection rules prevent reporting outcomes with <5 cases per cell (limits full assessment of rare events); (3) paternity not confirmed in MBR data (though previous work suggests same couple); (4) GDM screening not uniform (risk-factor based before 2008; universal from 2008), potentially affecting GDM detection rate.
- **Other notes**: This is a case–control study with 1:4 matching — a strong design for controlling major confounders (age, parity). Key finding: gestational hypertension (not preeclampsia overall) was significantly elevated. GDM elevated in crude analysis but lost significance after BMI adjustment. Placental problems composite and manual removal of placenta significantly elevated. Secondary RPL sub-analysis showed SGA more common (RR 2.50). Women with RPL used significantly more LMWH prophylaxis (9.4% vs. 2.4%), more outpatient visits, and had longer hospital stay — reflecting higher care intensity. This is the most recent study in the collection (accepted December 2024).
