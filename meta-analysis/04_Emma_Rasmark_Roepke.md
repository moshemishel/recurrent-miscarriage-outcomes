# Rasmark Roepke, 2021 — Meta-Analysis Extraction

## Group A: Study Characteristics
- **Authors**: Emma Rasmark Roepke, Ole Bjarne Christiansen, Karin Källén, Stefan R. Hansson
- **Year**: 2021 (published January 6, 2021; received November 15, 2020)
- **Journal**: Journal of Clinical Medicine (MDPI; J. Clin. Med. 2021, 10, 179; DOI: https://doi.org/10.3390/jcm10020179)
- **Country**: Sweden
- **Data source**: Swedish Medical Birth Register (MFR) — contains data on >97% of all births in Sweden since 1973; Swedish National Patient Register (NPR) — includes all inpatient care since 1987 and outpatient visits since 2001; registers linked via unique personal identity numbers; provided by the National Board of Health and Welfare (NBHW)
- **Study design**: Retrospective register-based cohort study
- **Study period**: 2003–2012 (RPL diagnoses in NPR); births registered in MFR during same period

## Group B: Population
- **Total N**: 62,381 pregnancies (4,971 RPL + 57,410 reference)
- **N RM group**: 4,971 first subsequent pregnancies by women with RPL (diagnosed ICD-10 codes N96.9 or O26.2 in NPR)
- **N control group**: 57,410 pregnancies by women without RPL (random selection of ~5% of all Swedish childbirths in study period)
- **Inclusion criteria**: RPL group: women registered with ICD-10 codes N96.9 or O26.2 in NPR during 2003–2012 with childbirths in MFR; only singleton pregnancies; only first subsequent childbirth after RPL diagnosis (childbirths before the miscarriages excluded). Reference group: women without RPL diagnosis with singleton childbirths in MFR during 2003–2012. Primary RPL (n=2,334, 47%) and secondary RPL (n=2,637, 53%) identified separately.
- **Exclusion criteria**: Multiple pregnancies; childbirths before the RPL-defining miscarriages; non-singleton pregnancies
- **Maternal age RM group**: <25 yr: 6.1%; 25–29 yr: 18.2%; 30–34 yr: 32.9%; 35–39 yr: 30.2%; >40 yr: 12.6%
- **Maternal age control group**: <25 yr: 20.2%; 25–29 yr: 27.1%; 30–34 yr: 29.7%; 35–39 yr: 18.3%; >40 yr: 4.7%; p < 0.001 (RPL women were older)
- **BMI RM group**: <18.5: 1.3%; 18.5–24.9: 49.3%; 25.0–29.9: 26.4%; >30.0: 14.0%; missing: 9.0%
- **BMI control group**: <18.5: 2.5%; 18.5–24.9: 52.2%; 25.0–29.9: 21.2%; >30.0: 9.6%; missing: 14.6%; p < 0.001 (RPL higher BMI)
- **Parity**: RPL group: parity 1 (47.0%), parity 2 (36.3%), parity 3 (11.5%), parity 4+ (5.2%); reference group: parity 1 (42.7%), parity 2 (35.8%), parity 3 (14.0%), parity 4+ (7.5%); p < 0.001
- **ART/IVF rate**: RPL group 7.1% vs. reference 2.3%; p < 0.001
- **Smoking**: RPL group 6.0% vs. reference 10.1%; p < 0.001 (RPL women less likely to smoke)

## Group C: Exposure & Control Definition
- **RM threshold**: ≥3 consecutive miscarriages (the traditional Swedish/German/Austrian/Swiss/British RCOG definition used in Sweden; ICD-10 codes N96.9 or O26.2)
- **Type of losses included**: Consecutive miscarriages occurring before fetus reaches viability; ICD-coded diagnoses. Positive predictive value of RPL diagnosis in NPR: 85% (95% CI: 78–89%).
- **Gestational age cutoff for "miscarriage"**: Before viability (not specified as a fixed week in methods; fetal death after 22 completed GWs defined as stillbirth)
- **Dose-response analysis**: No — only RPL (≥3 losses) vs. no RPL; subgroup analysis comparing primary vs. secondary RPL
- **Control group definition**: Random selection of women without RPL diagnosis who had singleton childbirths registered in MFR during 2003–2012; approximately 5% of all Swedish childbirths in the study period. Primary RPL compared with nulliparous reference; secondary RPL compared with multiparous reference.

## Group D: Outcomes & Methods
- **All outcomes measured**: Pre-eclampsia (ICD-10 O14–O15: BP >140/90 + proteinuria >0.3 g/24h or dipstick +1 on ≥2 occasions after 20 weeks; subclassified as mild/moderate and severe); stillbirth (fetal death after 22 completed GWs until delivery; before July 2008: after 28 GWs); SGA birth (birth weight <2 SDs below mean for GWs per growth and development record charts; both liveborn and stillborn included); preterm birth (<37 completed GWs; subclassified as 32–36 GWs and <32 GWs; further subclassified as spontaneous vs. iatrogenic); placental abruption (ICD-10 O45: separation of placenta from maternal uterine attachment). Each outcome further subdivided into preterm and term presentation.
- **Effect measure**: AOR (adjusted odds ratio) — note: "since there were low initial risk of the obstetrical outcomes, ORs could be used equivalent to relative risk"
- **Statistical method**: Stepwise unconditional multiple logistic regression; univariable ORs also reported; factors with univariable p < 0.2 entered into multiple model then restricted to p < 0.2
- **Confounders adjusted**: Outcome-specific: pre-eclampsia adjusted for age, parity, assisted conception, BMI, mother's country of birth, chronic hypertension, pregestational diabetes, smoking; stillbirth adjusted for age, parity, BMI, smoking; SGA adjusted for age, parity, BMI, smoking, mother's country of birth, chronic hypertension; preterm birth adjusted for age, parity, BMI, smoking; placental abruption adjusted for age, parity, BMI, smoking, chronic hypertension. Missing BMI values imputed with mean.

## Group E: Results

### Pre-eclampsia (any)
- **RM group**: 214/4,971 (4.3%)
- **Control group**: 1,275/57,410 (2.2%)
- **Crude OR (95% CI)**: 1.97 (1.70–2.28)
- **Adjusted AOR (95% CI)**: 1.45 (1.24–1.69)
- **p-value**: NR (significant — CIs exclude 1.0)
- **Subgroup/notes**: Mild/moderate PE: AOR 1.39 (1.17–1.68); severe PE: AOR 1.57 (1.20–2.07). PE with preterm birth: AOR 2.26 (1.70–3.01); PE at term: AOR 1.26 (1.05–1.51). Primary RPL: AOR 1.45 (1.20–1.75); secondary RPL: AOR 1.42 (1.10–1.85).

### Stillbirth (overall)
- **RM group**: 32/4,971 (0.6%)
- **Control group**: 232/57,410 (0.4%)
- **Crude OR (95% CI)**: 1.60 (1.10–2.31)
- **Adjusted AOR (95% CI)**: 1.38 (0.95–2.02)
- **p-value**: NR (AOR not significant — CI includes 1.0)
- **Subgroup/notes**: Preterm stillbirth (<37 GWs): crude OR 2.21 (1.42–3.42), AOR 1.92 (1.22–3.02) — significant; term stillbirth: crude OR 0.87 (0.43–1.79), AOR 0.76 (0.37–1.57) — not significant. Risk increase only for preterm stillbirth. Primary RPL stillbirth: AOR 1.36 (0.76–1.41) NS; secondary RPL: AOR 1.45 (0.87–2.40) NS.

### SGA birth (overall, birth weight <2 SD)
- **RM group**: 171/4,971 (3.4%)
- **Control group**: 1,409/57,410 (2.5%)
- **Crude OR (95% CI)**: 1.42 (1.21–1.66)
- **Adjusted AOR (95% CI)**: 1.25 (1.06–1.47)
- **p-value**: NR (significant)
- **Subgroup/notes**: SGA with preterm: AOR 2.00 (1.54–2.60) — significant; SGA at term: AOR 0.97 (0.78–1.20) — not significant. Risk increase only for preterm SGA. Primary RPL: AOR 1.15 (0.92–1.43) NS; secondary RPL: AOR 1.44 (1.11–1.88) significant.

### Preterm birth (<37 GWs, overall)
- **RM group**: 496/4,971 (10%)
- **Control group**: 2,882/57,410 (5.0%)
- **Crude OR (95% CI)**: 2.10 (1.90–2.32)
- **Adjusted AOR (95% CI)**: 1.95 (1.76–2.16)
- **p-value**: NR (significant)
- **Subgroup/notes**: Very preterm (<32 GWs): crude OR 4.59 (3.80–5.56), AOR 3.86 (3.16–4.72); moderately preterm (32–36 GWs): crude OR 1.70 (1.51–1.91), AOR 1.62 (1.43–1.82). Spontaneous preterm: AOR 1.82 (1.59–2.08); iatrogenic preterm: AOR 2.16 (1.85–2.53). Primary RPL <37 GWs: AOR 1.85 (1.60–2.13); <32 GWs: AOR 4.14 (3.20–5.37). Secondary RPL <37 GWs: AOR 2.07 (1.78–2.40); <32 GWs: AOR 3.37 (2.44–4.66).

### Placental abruption (overall)
- **RM group**: 50/4,971 (1.0%)
- **Control group**: 210/57,410 (0.4%)
- **Crude OR (95% CI)**: 2.77 (2.03–3.77)
- **Adjusted AOR (95% CI)**: 2.45 (1.78–3.38)
- **p-value**: NR (significant)
- **Subgroup/notes**: Placental abruption with preterm: AOR 2.47 (1.62–3.76); placental abruption at term: AOR 2.42 (1.49–1.96) [note: upper CI as reported in paper]. Primary RPL: AOR 2.48 (1.57–3.92); secondary RPL: AOR 2.42 (1.54–3.79).

## Group F: Quality Notes
- **Data source quality**: Large national register-based population, strongest study design for population-based questions. MFR covers >97% of all Swedish births; NPR covers all inpatient care since 1987 and outpatient since 2001. Linked via unique personal identity numbers. Underreporting of inpatient NPR data estimated <1%. PPV of pre-eclampsia diagnosis in MFR: 93%. PPV of RPL diagnosis in NPR: 85% (95% CI: 78–89%); negative predictive value not estimable.
- **Confounder adjustment**: Yes — multivariable logistic regression with outcome-specific confounder sets. Key confounders addressed: maternal age, BMI, smoking, parity, assisted reproduction, chronic hypertension, pre-gestational diabetes, country of birth. Missing BMI handled by mean imputation (~10% missing, low impact). Missing smoking handled as separate category.
- **Outcome validation**: ICD-10 codes (N96.9 or O26.2 for RPL; O14–O15 for pre-eclampsia; O45 for placental abruption; check boxes in MFR for stillbirth). Pre-eclampsia PPV validated at 93%.
- **Bias acknowledged by authors**: Yes — (1) PPV of RPL diagnosis 85%, meaning ~15% false positives potentially diluting associations toward null; (2) missing BMI for ~10% handled by imputation; (3) missing details on delivery start for 4.7% assumed spontaneous; (4) missing SGA data assumed AGA for 313 births (0.5%); (5) study focused on only first subsequent childbirth — all births post-RPL could be of interest; (6) no information on preterm PROM available.
- **Other notes**: This is the largest study in this meta-analysis (n=62,381). Key methodological strength: subgroup analyses comparing primary vs. secondary RPL with matched parity reference groups (nulliparous controls for primary, multiparous controls for secondary RPL). A critical finding is that placenta-associated disorders are strongly associated with preterm birth — outcomes that appear insignificant at term become strongly significant when birth is preterm. The RM definition (≥3 losses) is stricter than many other studies in this collection. The reference group is randomly selected from the population (not matched). Country of birth (Nordic vs. non-Nordic) included as confounder proxy for ethnicity.
