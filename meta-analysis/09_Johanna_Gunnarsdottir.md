# Gunnarsdottir, 2014 — Meta-Analysis Extraction

## Group A: Study Characteristics
- **Authors**: Johanna Gunnarsdottir, Olof Stephansson, Sven Cnattingius, Helena Åkerud, Anna-Karin Wikström
- **Year**: 2014
- **Journal**: American Journal of Obstetrics & Gynecology
- **Country**: Sweden
- **Data source**: Swedish Medical Birth Register — a population-based national registry containing data on >98% of all births in Sweden since 1973, linked to the Register of Total Population and the Education Register
- **Study design**: Retrospective cohort (population-based)
- **Study period**: 1995–2009

## Group B: Population
- **Total N**: 619,587 primiparous women
- **N RM group**: 11,410 (2 miscarriages) + 3,823 (≥3 miscarriages) = 15,233 with ≥2 miscarriages; 68,185 with 1 miscarriage also analyzed as a separate tier
- **N control group**: 536,169 (no prior miscarriage)
- **Inclusion criteria**: Women giving birth to their first singleton infant at 22 weeks of gestation or later during 1995–2009
- **Exclusion criteria**: Multiple pregnancies; births before 22 weeks gestation; non-primiparous women (parity ≥1 at delivery). Specific sub-analyses excluded additional births (e.g., stillbirth analysis restricted to ≥28 weeks, n = 617,708; SGA analysis excluded missing birthweight, n = 615,130; spontaneous PTB analysis excluded missing onset-of-labor data, n = 596,659)
- **Maternal age RM group**: Higher than controls; ≥35 years: 14.5% (1 mc), 21.8% (2 mc), 29.0% (≥3 mc) vs 8.7% (controls)
- **Maternal age control group**: <25 years: 26.6%; 25–29: 38.0%; 30–34: 26.8%; ≥35: 8.7%
- **BMI RM group**: Higher than controls; ≥30 kg/m²: 9.9% (1 mc), 11.3% (2 mc), 13.7% (≥3 mc) vs 8.2% (controls)
- **BMI control group**: ≥30 kg/m²: 8.2%; data missing for 82,745 women
- **Parity**: All primiparous (first delivery ≥22 weeks)
- **ART/IVF rate**: IVF yes: 4.0% (1 mc), 5.9% (2 mc), 7.5% (≥3 mc) vs 2.3% (controls)
- **Smoking**: ≥10 cigarettes/day: 2.8% (1 mc), 3.1% (2 mc), 4.3% (≥3 mc) vs 2.2% (controls)

## Group C: Exposure & Control Definition
- **RM threshold**: Categorized as 0, 1, 2, and ≥3 prior miscarriages (dose-response design); no single threshold defining "RM"
- **Type of losses included**: Self-reported miscarriages (spontaneous pregnancy losses), regardless of gestational age, etiology, or whether consecutive; no biochemical-only losses specifically distinguished
- **Gestational age cutoff for "miscarriage"**: Not specified in the paper — self-reported; no gestational age cutoff for the loss is stated
- **Dose-response analysis**: Yes — four tiers: 0 miscarriages (reference), 1, 2, and ≥3 prior miscarriages
- **Control group definition**: Primiparous women with no prior miscarriage (n = 536,169), derived from the same national register during the same period

## Group D: Outcomes & Methods
- **All outcomes measured**:
  - Preeclampsia (ICD-9 642E–G; ICD-10 O14–O15): overall, preterm (<37 wks), and term (≥37 wks)
  - Stillbirth (fetal death ≥28 weeks; ICD codes not separately listed): overall, preterm, term
  - Small for gestational age (SGA): birthweight >2 SD below sex-specific Swedish fetal growth curve mean; live births only; overall, preterm, term
  - Placental abruption (ICD-9 641C; ICD-10 O45): overall, preterm, term
  - Spontaneous preterm birth (<37 weeks with spontaneous onset, including PPROM; SGA births excluded): overall, very preterm (<32 wks), moderately preterm (32–36 wks)
- **Effect measure**: OR (odds ratio)
- **Statistical method**: Unconditional logistic regression; no prior miscarriage = reference
- **Confounders adjusted**: Maternal age, early pregnancy BMI, height, smoking habits, country of birth, years of formal education, IVF, chronic hypertension, pregestational diabetes, hypothyroidism, systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE), fetal sex, year of birth

## Group E: Results

### Preeclampsia (overall)
- **RM group (≥3 mc)**: 223/3,823, 5.83%
- **Control group**: 22,915/536,169, 4.27%
- **Crude OR/RR (95% CI)**: NR
- **Adjusted aOR (95% CI)**: 1.23 (1.06–1.42) for ≥3 mc; 0.94 (0.85–1.04) for 2 mc; 0.98 (0.94–1.02) for 1 mc
- **p-value**: NR
- **Subgroup/notes**: Preterm preeclampsia (<37 wks): aOR 1.62 (1.24–2.11) for ≥3 mc; aOR 1.07 (0.88–1.30) for 2 mc. Term preeclampsia: aOR 1.17 (0.98–1.38) for ≥3 mc. Significant association only for ≥3 mc overall and preterm subgroup.

### Stillbirth (≥28 weeks)
- **RM group (≥3 mc)**: 26/3,823 (denominator ~3,812 adjusted), 0.69%
- **Control group**: 1,790/~536,000, 0.33%
- **Crude OR/RR (95% CI)**: NR
- **Adjusted aOR (95% CI)**: 1.62 (1.05–2.51) for ≥3 mc; 1.02 (0.73–1.42) for 2 mc; 0.96 (0.83–1.12) for 1 mc
- **p-value**: NR
- **Subgroup/notes**: Preterm stillbirth: aOR 2.25 (1.23–4.10) for ≥3 mc; term stillbirth: aOR 1.29 (0.69–2.42) for ≥3 mc. Significant only for ≥3 mc overall and preterm subgroup. Total population for stillbirth analysis = 617,708.

### SGA (small for gestational age, overall)
- **RM group (≥3 mc)**: 192/3,775 (denominator approx), 5.09%
- **Control group**: 17,119/~531,645 (denominator approx), 3.22%
- **Crude OR/RR (95% CI)**: NR
- **Adjusted aOR (95% CI)**: 1.38 (1.18–1.62) for ≥3 mc; 1.06 (0.95–1.18) for 2 mc; 0.98 (0.93–1.03) for 1 mc
- **p-value**: NR
- **Subgroup/notes**: Preterm SGA: aOR 2.21 (1.69–2.88) for ≥3 mc; aOR 1.32 (1.07–1.62) for 2 mc. Term SGA: aOR 1.20 (0.99–1.46) for ≥3 mc. Total population for SGA = 615,130.

### Placental Abruption (overall)
- **RM group (≥3 mc)**: 31/3,823, 0.81%
- **Control group**: 2,225/536,169, 0.41%
- **Crude OR/RR (95% CI)**: NR
- **Adjusted aOR (95% CI)**: 1.82 (1.25–2.66) for ≥3 mc; 1.30 (1.00–1.70) for 2 mc; 1.03 (0.90–1.18) for 1 mc
- **p-value**: NR
- **Subgroup/notes**: Preterm abruption: aOR 2.23 (1.37–3.62) for ≥3 mc; aOR 1.45 (1.01–2.08) for 2 mc. Term abruption: aOR 1.49 (0.82–2.71) for ≥3 mc; aOR 1.17 (0.78–1.75) for 2 mc; term abruption for 1 mc: aOR 1.19 (1.00–1.42).

### Spontaneous Preterm Birth (<37 weeks, overall)
- **RM group (≥3 mc)**: 232/~3,596 (denominator from 596,659 total), 6.45%
- **Control group**: 22,738/~516,604 (denominator approx), 4.40%
- **Crude OR/RR (95% CI)**: NR
- **Adjusted aOR (95% CI)**: 1.50 (1.30–1.74) for ≥3 mc; 1.18 (1.08–1.30) for 2 mc; 1.04 (1.00–1.09) for 1 mc
- **p-value**: NR
- **Subgroup/notes**: Very preterm (<32 wks): aOR 2.60 (1.86–3.64) for ≥3 mc; aOR 1.84 (1.46–2.33) for 2 mc; aOR 1.28 (1.13–1.45) for 1 mc. Moderately preterm (32–36 wks): aOR 1.38 (1.18–1.62) for ≥3 mc; aOR 1.11 (1.00–1.23) for 2 mc; aOR 1.02 (0.97–1.06) for 1 mc. Total population = 596,659. Clear dose-response for spontaneous PTB across all tiers.

## Group F: Quality Notes
- **Data source quality**: High — national registry with >98% birth coverage; validated ICD codes; gestational age by ultrasound in 97% of women
- **Confounder adjustment**: Yes — extensive: adjusted for maternal age, BMI, height, smoking, country of birth, education, IVF, chronic hypertension, pregestational diabetes, hypothyroidism, SLE, fetal sex, year of birth. Not adjusted for thrombophilia or PCOS (acknowledged limitation).
- **Outcome validation**: ICD-9 and ICD-10 codes from national registry; preeclampsia diagnosis previously validated (93% of coded cases confirmed)
- **Bias acknowledged by authors**: Yes — self-reported miscarriage data (no gestational age or etiology of losses); lack of data on induced abortions; no adjustment for thrombophilia or PCOS; effect measure modification by age tested and found non-significant
- **Other notes**: Largest study in the collection (N > 619,000); restricted to primiparous women to avoid confounding by parity; dose-response analysis is a major strength; no single RM threshold — results presented across all four tiers. Women with 2 mc show intermediate risk; ≥3 mc show strongest associations especially for preterm disorders.
