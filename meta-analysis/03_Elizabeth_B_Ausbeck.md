# Ausbeck, 2021 — Meta-Analysis Extraction

## Group A: Study Characteristics
- **Authors**: Elizabeth B. Ausbeck, Christina Blanchard, Alan T. Tita, Jeff M. Szychowski, Lorie Harper
- **Year**: 2021 (published online June 30, 2020; print volume 38, No. 1, 2021)
- **Journal**: American Journal of Perinatology (Vol. 38, No. 1, pp. 10–15; DOI: https://doi.org/10.1055/s-0040-1713650)
- **Country**: United States (Birmingham, Alabama)
- **Data source**: Single academic institution (University of Alabama at Birmingham Hospital) perinatal database containing searchable medical records; institutional review board approved
- **Study design**: Retrospective cohort study
- **Study period**: January 1, 2008 through December 31, 2017

## Group B: Population
- **Total N**: 17,670 eligible women (from 22,447 women with 29,356 deliveries of nonanomalous singletons at ≥20 weeks; 4,777 excluded for missing data)
- **N RM group**: 235 (1.3% of eligible women) with ≥2 consecutive SABs at <12 weeks
- **N control group**: 17,435 women with ≤1 prior SAB at <12 weeks
- **Inclusion criteria**: Singleton pregnancy at ≥20 weeks' gestational age; delivered at UAB Hospital; pregnancy history available. Only the earliest subsequent pregnancy reaching 20 weeks included for women with multiple deliveries.
- **Exclusion criteria**: Prenatal care at institution but delivery elsewhere; known fetal anomaly (markers of aneuploidy such as increased nuchal thickness, echogenic intracardiac focus, hypoplastic nasal bone not considered structural anomalies and not excluded). Women excluded if unable to verify gestational age of prior losses or that losses occurred consecutively.
- **Maternal age RM group**: 29.9 ± 5.5 years (mean ± SD)
- **Maternal age control group**: 25.6 ± 6.3 years (mean ± SD); p < 0.01
- **BMI RM group**: 31.6 ± 13.4 kg/m² (available for n=215 of 235)
- **BMI control group**: 28.7 ± 10.3 kg/m² (available for n=15,209 of 17,435); p < 0.01
- **Parity**: RM group: nulliparous 47 (20.0%), parous 188 (80.0%); control group: nulliparous 9,815 (56.3%), parous 7,620 (43.7%); p < 0.01
- **ART/IVF rate**: NR
- **Smoking**: NR

## Group C: Exposure & Control Definition
- **RM threshold**: ≥2 consecutive spontaneous abortions (ASRM definition used; authors note ASRM recommends evaluation after 2 or more consecutive losses)
- **Type of losses included**: Consecutive spontaneous abortions only; losses must have been verified as consecutive. Non-consecutive losses excluded.
- **Gestational age cutoff for "miscarriage"**: <12 weeks' gestational age (explicitly restricted to exclude mid-trimester losses, which may have different etiologies such as cervical insufficiency or preterm labor)
- **Dose-response analysis**: No — only ≥2 vs. ≤1 comparison; number of losses within ≥2 group not analyzed separately
- **Control group definition**: Women with a history of ≤1 SAB at <12 weeks' gestation; includes women with 0 prior SABs and women with exactly 1 prior SAB at <12 weeks

## Group D: Outcomes & Methods
- **All outcomes measured**: Primary: preterm birth (PTB) <37 weeks. Secondary: gestational age at delivery (mean weeks); gestational diabetes mellitus (GDM; Carpenter–Coustan criteria, screening/diagnostic testing at 24–28 weeks); small for gestational age birth weight (SGA; <10th percentile for gestational age by Brenner's nomogram); hypertensive diseases of pregnancy (gestational hypertension and preeclampsia, as recorded by delivery physician); fetal demise; cesarean delivery; neonatal composite outcome (5-minute Apgar score <5, perinatal death, NICU admission). Also reported individually: 5-minute Apgar score <5; perinatal death (sum of infant deaths <28 days of age and fetal deaths ≥20 weeks); NICU admission.
- **Effect measure**: AOR (adjusted odds ratio); also crude counts and percentages for all outcomes; mean ± SD for continuous outcomes
- **Statistical method**: Multivariable logistic regression for binary outcomes; Student's t-test for continuous outcomes; chi-squared or Fisher's exact test for categorical variables. Each outcome adjusted for a different set of confounders (outcome-specific models).
- **Confounders adjusted**: Outcome-specific adjustment sets: PTB adjusted for prior PTB, age, race, hypertension, diabetes; GDM adjusted for parity, age, BMI, race, primary source of payment; SGA adjusted for parity, BMI, race, hypertension, diabetes; hypertensive diseases adjusted for parity, age, BMI, race, primary source of payment, diabetes; fetal demise adjusted for parity and primary source of payment; cesarean adjusted for parity, age, BMI, race, primary source of payment, hypertension, diabetes; neonatal composite adjusted for parity, age, race, hypertension, diabetes; 5-min Apgar <5 adjusted for parity, race, hypertension, diabetes; perinatal death adjusted for parity, race, hypertension; NICU admission adjusted for parity, age, BMI, race, hypertension, diabetes.

## Group E: Results

### Preterm birth (<37 weeks) — Primary outcome
- **RM group**: 46/235 (19.6%)
- **Control group**: 2,436/17,435 (14.0%)
- **Crude OR/RR (95% CI)**: NR (crude p = 0.01)
- **Adjusted AOR (95% CI)**: 0.91 (0.62–1.33)
- **p-value**: p = 0.01 (crude); AOR not significant after adjustment
- **Subgroup/notes**: After adjustment for prior PTB, age, race, hypertension, and diabetes, the apparent increase in PTB was no longer significant. The crude difference is largely attributable to confounders, particularly prior PTB (25.1% in RM group vs. 9.0% in controls, p < 0.01).

### Gestational age at delivery (mean)
- **RM group**: 37.8 ± 3.4 weeks
- **Control group**: 38.6 ± 2.9 weeks
- **Crude OR/RR (95% CI)**: NA (continuous variable)
- **Adjusted AOR (95% CI)**: Not modeled
- **p-value**: p < 0.01
- **Subgroup/notes**: Significant difference in mean gestational age even though adjusted PTB was not significant

### Gestational diabetes mellitus (GDM)
- **RM group**: 29/235 (12.3%)
- **Control group**: 1,154/17,435 (6.6%)

- **p-value**: p < 0.01 (crude); AOR significant
- **Subgroup/notes**: Adjusted for parity, age, BMI, race, primary source of payment. Authors note multiple comparisons raise possibility of chance finding. This was the only secondary outcome reaching significance after adjustment.

## Group F: Quality Notes
- **Data source quality**: Single academic institution perinatal database; detailed medical records with ability to verify gestational age and consecutiveness of losses. Restricting to verified consecutive losses <12 weeks resulted in a smaller-than-expected RM cohort (1.3% vs. expected 2–3%), reflecting the strict inclusion criteria. Data quality should be high given institutional database with physician-coded records. Limitation: single center, predominantly Black population (49.4% RM group, 49.2% controls), may limit generalizability.
- **Confounder adjustment**: Yes — comprehensive multivariable logistic regression with outcome-specific confounder sets. Key confounders included: prior PTB (critical confounder for PTB outcome — 25.1% vs. 9.0%), maternal age (29.9 vs. 25.6 years), parity (80% parous in RM vs. 43.7%), BMI, race, payer status, chronic hypertension, pregestational diabetes.
- **Outcome validation**: Physician-recorded in institutional perinatal database; GDM by Carpenter–Coustan criteria; SGA by Brenner's nomogram; hypertensive diseases as recorded by delivery physician (no ICD code validation described).
- **Bias acknowledged by authors**: Yes — (1) RPL defined by patient self-report, possible misclassification biasing toward null; (2) RM cohort smaller than expected due to strict inclusion criteria (gestational age verification and consecutiveness); (3) etiology of RPL unknown; (4) multiple comparisons raise possibility of chance finding for GDM result; (5) confounders from older maternal age and comorbidities.
- **Other notes**: Important distinction: control group includes women with 0 or 1 prior SAB (not a 0-SAB-only control). This dilutes the comparison vs. a true nulliparous control. The RM definition (≥2 consecutive, <12 weeks) is narrower than most other studies (which use ≥3 losses or include second-trimester losses). The apparently increased crude PTB (19.6% vs. 14.0%) becomes non-significant after adjustment, highlighting importance of confounders (particularly prior PTB). The increased GDM (AOR 1.69) is the study's main positive finding.
