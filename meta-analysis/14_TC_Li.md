# TC Li, 2023 — Meta-Analysis Extraction

## Group A: Study Characteristics
- **Authors**: TC Li, BHK Yip, X Chen
- **Year**: 2023
- **Journal**: Hong Kong Medical Journal (Hong Kong Med J 2023;29(Suppl 1):S44–6)
- **Country**: Hong Kong SAR, China
- **Data source**: Clinical Data Analysis and Reporting System (CDARS), Hong Kong — electronic medical records from the Department of Obstetrics and Gynaecology, Prince of Wales Hospital, The Chinese University of Hong Kong. CDARS provides clinical information for management, audit, research, and data analysis across Hong Kong public hospitals.
- **Study design**: Retrospective cohort study (database study)
- **Study period**: January 2000 – December 2019 (20-year period)

## Group B: Population
- **Total N**: 111,124 women with singleton pregnancies
- **N RM group**: 3,112 (2.8%) with a history of two or more miscarriages; of these, 697 (22.4%) had primary RM and 2,415 (77.6%) had secondary RM
- **N control group**: 108,012 (97.2%) without a history of RM
- **Inclusion criteria**: All women with singleton pregnancy admitted between January 2000 and December 2019 to the Department of Obstetrics and Gynaecology, Prince of Wales Hospital, CUHK. All women underwent maternal and paternal karyotyping, basal hormone profiling, prothrombotic screening and antiphospholipid antibody test, thyroid function and thyroid antibodies tests, and ultrasonography (for the RM group). All other women without a history of RM during the same period included for comparison.
- **Exclusion criteria**: Multiple pregnancies (women with multiple pregnancies excluded)
- **Maternal age RM group**: Age distribution: <20 years 0.7% (n=21), 20–29 years 22.2% (n=691), 30–39 years 63.2% (n=1,979), >40 years 13.5% (n=421). Significantly older than controls (p < 0.001).
- **Maternal age control group**: Age distribution: <20 years 5.1% (n=5,562), 20–29 years 35.0% (n=37,804), 30–39 years 53.0% (n=57,246), >40 years 6.9% (n=7,400)
- **BMI RM group**: Mean ± SD: 22.3 ± 3.8 kg/m²
- **BMI control group**: Mean ± SD: 22.9 ± 4.2 kg/m² (p = 0.058; not significantly different)
- **Parity**: Primary RM (no prior viable pregnancy): 697 (22.4%); secondary RM (≥1 prior viable pregnancy): 2,415 (77.6%)
- **ART/IVF rate**: Assisted conception: 560/3,112 (18.0%) in RM group vs 11,080/108,012 (10.0%) in control group (p < 0.001)
- **Smoking**: NR

## Group C: Exposure & Control Definition
- **RM threshold**: ≥2 miscarriages ("two or more miscarriages") — consistent with ESHRE 2017 guideline definition
- **Type of losses included**: History of two or more miscarriages; specific type (biochemical vs. clinical, consecutive vs. non-consecutive) not explicitly stated; all women underwent standardized investigation protocol (karyotyping, hormone profiling, thrombophilic screening, antiphospholipid antibody testing, thyroid tests, ultrasonography)
- **Gestational age cutoff for "miscarriage"**: "before the 24th week of gestation" (stated in Introduction: "Miscarriage is estimated to occur in 11% to 20% of all clinically recognised pregnancies before the 24th week of gestation")
- **Dose-response analysis**: No — binary comparison (RM history yes vs. no); subgroup analysis by primary vs. secondary RM type
- **Control group definition**: All other women without a history of RM with singleton pregnancies admitted during the same period at the same institution (n = 108,012)

## Group D: Outcomes & Methods
- **All outcomes measured**:
  - Obstetric outcomes: gestational hypertension, preeclampsia, eclampsia, antepartum haemorrhage, gestational diabetes, operative vaginal delivery, caesarean section
  - Perinatal outcomes: infant sex, preterm birth (gestational age at delivery), small for gestational age (SGA), large for gestational age (LGA), Apgar score <7 at 1 minute, Apgar score <7 at 5 minutes, admission to neonatal unit, perinatal death (from all causes), congenital anomalies
  - Gestational hypertensive disorders (combined category)
  - Mode of delivery
- **Effect measure**: OR (odds ratio) and adjusted OR with 95% CI
- **Statistical method**: Student's t-test, ANOVA, or non-parametric test for continuous variables; chi-squared test or Fisher's exact test for categorical variables; univariable and multivariable stepwise logistic regression; SPSS version 25
- **Confounders adjusted**: Maternal age, type of pregnancy (natural or assisted conception), and gestational age at booking. Multivariable stepwise logistic regression used.

## Group F: Quality Notes
- **Data source quality**: Moderate-high — large dataset (N > 111,000) from a single tertiary hospital with 20-year data span; CDARS electronic medical records with high coding accuracy validated by individual record review. As a tertiary referral hospital, the control group may be skewed toward higher pregnancy risk (acknowledged by authors).
- **Confounder adjustment**: Partial — adjusted for maternal age, type of pregnancy (natural vs. assisted conception), and gestational age at booking only. BMI (not significantly different between groups) not adjusted; other potential confounders not adjusted.
- **Outcome validation**: ICD-9 Clinical Modification (CM) coding; "data validation by reviewing individual electronic medical records demonstrated high coding accuracy for diagnosis"
- **Bias acknowledged by authors**: Yes — Prince of Wales Hospital is a tertiary referral centre so control group may be skewed toward higher risk (would underestimate true differences); study population mainly Chinese (may not generalize to other ethnicities); retrospective design with potentially missing clinical parameters (e.g., detailed paternal information)
- **Other notes**: This is an abridged secondary publication (conference supplement) — full report available from the Health and Medical Research Fund (HMRF) website (project 07180226). The study is from the same research group (TC Li) as the Jivraj 2001 paper but uses a much larger CDARS database cohort rather than a clinic-based cohort. The population is predominantly Chinese. The study uses the ESHRE ≥2 miscarriage definition (not ≥3). Four significant adjusted outcomes: caesarean section, preterm birth, SGA, and perinatal death.
