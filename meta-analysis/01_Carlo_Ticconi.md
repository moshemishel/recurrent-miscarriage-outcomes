# Ticconi, 2020 — Meta-Analysis Extraction

## Group A: Study Characteristics
- **Authors**: Carlo Ticconi, Adalgisa Pietropolli, Monia Specchia, Elena Nicastri, Carlo Chiaramonte, Emilio Piccione, Giovanni Scambia, Nicoletta Di Simone
- **Year**: 2020
- **Journal**: Journal of Clinical Medicine (MDPI)
- **Country**: Italy (Rome)
- **Data source**: Prospective clinical follow-up at two university hospitals (Policlinico Tor Vergata University Hospital and Università Cattolica / Policlinico Gemelli Hospital, Rome). RPL women were followed at dedicated RPL Units; controls were followed in Low-Risk Obstetric Clinics.
- **Study design**: Prospective cohort study
- **Study period**: January 2017 to January 2020 (enrollment); follow-up until delivery

## Group B: Population
- **Total N**: 1092 singleton pregnancies followed to delivery (431 in RPL group, 661 controls)
- **N RM group**: 431
- **N control group**: 661
- **Inclusion criteria (RPL group)**: Non-pregnant women with RPL enrolled at their first visit for investigation of possible causes/risk factors of RPL; women who subsequently became pregnant were followed during gestation. RPL defined per ESHRE 2017 Guidelines (spontaneous loss of ≥2 pregnancies before 24 weeks). Women with pre-existing diabetes and hypertension before pregnancy were excluded.
- **Inclusion criteria (control group)**: Pregnant normal women with low-risk pregnancy who had at least one uncomplicated pregnancy at term, with no previous pregnancy loss; selected consecutively. Required at least two pregnancies at term without any losses. Absence of any pre-existing medical conditions, no previous gynecologic surgery, no assumption of drugs before pregnancy.
- **Exclusion criteria**: Pre-existing diabetes and hypertension; multiple pregnancies; for controls: pre-existing medical conditions, previous gynecologic surgery, drug use before pregnancy.
- **Maternal age RM group**: 35.83 ± 5.95 years (mean ± SD)
- **Maternal age control group**: 31.70 ± 5.82 years (mean ± SD); p < 0.001 vs. RPL
- **BMI RM group**: 24.51 ± 4.66 kg/m²
- **BMI control group**: 23.41 ± 4.78 kg/m²; p < 0.001 vs. RPL
- **Parity**: NR (controls required ≥2 prior term pregnancies; RPL group: primary RPL 284/431 (65.89%), secondary RPL 147/431 (34.11%))
- **ART/IVF rate**: NR
- **Smoking**: RPL group 56 (13%); control group 126 (19%); p < 0.05

## Group C: Exposure & Control Definition
- **RM threshold**: ≥2 pregnancy losses (per ESHRE 2017 Guidelines); study population included women with 2 losses (48.5%) and ≥3 losses (51.5%)
- **Type of losses included**: Spontaneous pregnancy loss (miscarriage defined as spontaneous PL occurring before 24 weeks gestation); both explained and unexplained RPL included
- **Gestational age cutoff for "miscarriage"**: Before 24 weeks of gestation (per ESHRE 2017 definition used)
- **Dose-response analysis**: Yes — women with RPL stratified by number of losses: 2 losses (n=209), 3 losses, >3 losses. OR for ≥3 losses vs. 2 losses (overall complications): OR = 1.269; 95% CI: 1.112–2.386, p < 0.02. Table 4 reports ORs for each specific complication by tier (2 losses, 3 losses, >3 losses), all vs. controls.
- **Control group definition**: Normal healthy pregnant women with at least one uncomplicated pregnancy at term and no previous pregnancy losses, with low-risk pregnancy; followed in Low-Risk Obstetric Clinics; all had ≥2 prior term pregnancies without losses

## Group D: Outcomes & Methods
- **All outcomes measured**: Threatened miscarriage (abnormal vaginal bleeding and abdominal pain before 24 weeks in otherwise ongoing pregnancy); spontaneous miscarriage (spontaneous PL before 24 weeks); cervical insufficiency (CL < 25 mm by TVU or cervical changes on physical exam before 24 weeks); chromosomal/genetic abnormalities (any detected alteration of fetal karyotype and/or DNA); fetal anomalies (any structural/morphological abnormality detected by ultrasound); oligohydramnios (AFV < 5th percentile for GA, or AFI < 5 cm or MDP < 2 cm); polyhydramnios (deepest vertical pocket > 8 cm or AFI > 24 cm); fetal growth restriction (fetus with UEFW 5–10th percentile for GA, calculated using IGC per Snijders and Nicolaides); intrauterine fetal death (fetal death at ≥24 weeks gestation); gestational diabetes mellitus (GDM, per WHO criteria); preeclampsia (per ACOG 2013); placenta previa/low-lying placenta (per RCOG criteria); abruptio placentae (premature separation of placenta before delivery); pregnancy-related liver disorders (hyperemesis gravidarum, intrahepatic cholestasis of pregnancy, acute fatty liver of pregnancy, HELLP syndrome, isolated abnormal liver enzymes, biliary obstruction by gallbladder stones); preterm PROM (premature rupture of membranes before 37 weeks)
- **Effect measure**: OR (odds ratio)
- **Statistical method**: Chi-square test; Student's t-test; Bravais–Pearson coefficient for correlations; factorial analysis for age and BMI effects (method of analysis of averages). No multivariable regression reported; crude ORs presented with factorial correction for age/BMI as Δ% effect size.
- **Confounders adjusted**: Age and BMI effects assessed separately via factorial analysis (reported as Δ% of total population); no formal multivariate regression adjustment reported. The Δ% values indicate the magnitude of age and BMI effects on each complication.

## Group F: Quality Notes
- **Data source quality**: Prospective observational design at two specialized university RPL units; controls from low-risk obstetric clinics of the same institutions. Data collected in a preconceived template and computerized database; data anonymized prior to analysis.
- **Confounder adjustment**: Partial — no formal multivariate regression. Age and BMI effects assessed separately via factorial analysis (Δ% method). Crude ORs presented. Age was significantly higher in RPL group (35.83 vs. 31.70 yrs) and BMI was higher (24.51 vs. 23.41), with demonstrated effects on several outcomes; this is a limitation.
- **Outcome validation**: Clinical diagnosis at two university hospitals by physicians following ESHRE/ACOG/RCOG/WHO criteria (as specified in Table 1 definitions). Not ICD coded.
- **Bias acknowledged by authors**: Yes — authors acknowledge that age and BMI were higher in RPL group and performed factorial analysis to estimate their effects. Also acknowledge loss to follow-up (13.7% of RPL women during pregnancy; 12.1% of controls). Incomplete postnatal genetic follow-up of newborns noted. RPL women followed in specialized units while controls in low-risk clinics (care differential). Control group required ≥2 prior term deliveries (multiparous), creating parity mismatch with RPL group.
- **Other notes**: Dose-response analysis by number of losses is a key strength. Subgroup analyses by explained/unexplained RPL and primary/secondary RPL performed. The control group selection (requiring ≥2 prior uncomplicated term pregnancies) means controls are inherently lower risk than general obstetric population, potentially inflating ORs. The preterm PROM OR of 51.4 (95% CI: 26.17–100.98) is unusually large and may reflect data entry or table formatting issues in the original paper; transcribed verbatim. Live birth rates: 86% in RPL vs. 97.2% in controls.
