# Ali, 2020 — Meta-Analysis Extraction

## Group A: Study Characteristics
- **Authors**: Nasloon Ali, Iffat Elbarazi, Saad Ghazal-Aswad, Fatima Al-Maskari, Rami H Al-Rifai, Abderrahim Oulhaj, Tom Loney, Luai A Ahmed
- **Year**: 2020
- **Journal**: International Journal of Women's Health (2020:12, pp. 1171–1179)
- **Country**: United Arab Emirates (Al Ain, UAE)
- **Data source**: Mutaba'ah Mother and Child Health Study — a prospective cohort study recruiting pregnant Emirati women from three major health institutions in Al Ain, UAE (Al Ain Hospital, Tawam Hospital, and a third site). Data from baseline survey and hospital medical records (first visit through postnatal discharge). Interim analysis of the Mutaba'ah Study.
- **Study design**: Prospective cohort study (interim analysis)
- **Study period**: Recruitment May 2017 – April 2019; follow-up until delivery

## Group B: Population
- **Total N**: 1,737 pregnant women with gravidity ≥2 prior to the current pregnancy (subset of 2,769 total Mutaba'ah participants who completed pregnancies at time of analysis)
- **N RM group**: 234 (13.5%) women with history of ≥2 consecutive miscarriages
- **N control group**: 1,503 (86.5%) women with gravidity ≥2 and no history of ≥2 consecutive losses
- **Inclusion criteria**: Emirati pregnant women aged ≥18 years, resident in Al Ain, able to provide informed consent, with gravidity ≥2 prior to the current pregnancy. Recruited during antenatal care visits at three major health institutions.
- **Exclusion criteria**: Primiparous women (n = 542) and women with only one previous pregnancy (gravida = 1, n = 490) excluded from this analysis. Women who could not complete the questionnaire or were not Emirati were ineligible for the parent study.
- **Maternal age RM group**: Mean ± SD: 33.7 ± 5.6 years
- **Maternal age control group**: Mean ± SD: 32.6 ± 5.2 years (p = 0.005)
- **BMI RM group**: Body weight at first visit: 72.9 ± 14.3 kg (p = 0.479 vs. controls, not significantly different). BMI as such not reported; body weight used as proxy.
- **BMI control group**: Body weight at first visit: 72.1 ± 14.7 kg
- **Parity**: RM group: mean number of children 3.3 ± 0.1; gravidity 6.2 ± 2.6. Control group: mean number of children 3.4 ± 0.05; gravidity 4.0 ± 2.0 (gravidity significantly higher in RM group, p < 0.001; parity not significantly different, p = 0.278)
- **ART/IVF rate**: Previous infertility treatment: 22.1% (RM group) vs 8.1% (control), p < 0.001. Information on ART in the current pregnancy was confidential and unavailable.
- **Smoking**: Paternal smoking status collected; maternal and paternal smoking captured in questionnaire but specific rates not reported for the two groups in the published results

## Group C: Exposure & Control Definition
- **RM threshold**: Primary analysis: ≥2 consecutive miscarriages (per ESHRE guidelines, ACOG, ASRM, and Dutch Society of Obstetrics and Gynecology). Supplementary analysis also performed using ≥3 consecutive losses (76 women identified out of 1,316 with gravidity ≥3).
- **Type of losses included**: Consecutive miscarriages. Defined as "all pregnancy losses from the time of conception (fertilized ovum) to neonate," including missed miscarriages, ectopic pregnancies, molar pregnancies, or therapeutic abortions (mentioned in Discussion). Medical records used to confirm exposure.
- **Gestational age cutoff for "miscarriage"**: Before the 20th week of gestation (per RM definition cited: "two consecutive miscarriages before the 20th week of gestation")
- **Dose-response analysis**: No — single binary threshold (RM yes/no) in primary analysis. Supplementary analysis repeated with ≥3 losses threshold.
- **Control group definition**: Women with gravidity ≥2 prior to the current pregnancy and no history of two or more consecutive miscarriages (n = 1,503)

## Group D: Outcomes & Methods
- **All outcomes measured**:
  - Caesarean section (spontaneous vaginal delivery, assisted vaginal delivery, or caesarean section)
  - Preterm delivery (<37 weeks gestation)
  - Very preterm delivery (<32 weeks gestation)
  - Preeclampsia (defined per medical record diagnosis)
  - Gestational diabetes mellitus (GDM): from medical records
  - Blood loss during delivery (continuous variable, mL)
- **Effect measure**: OR (crude) and aOR (adjusted odds ratio) with 95% CI
- **Statistical method**: Univariate and multivariate logistic regression; Student's t-test for continuous variables, Pearson Chi-square for categorical variables; Stata 16 (Stata Corp)
- **Confounders adjusted**: All models adjusted for age, gravidity, body mass (weight at first visit), and gestational age at delivery (note: preterm delivery model did not adjust for gestational age at delivery due to collinearity)

## Group E: Results

### Caesarean Section
- **RM group**: 34.2% (n = 234 total; ~80 women)
- **Control group**: 21.1% (n = 1,503 total; ~317 women)
- **Crude OR/RR (95% CI)**: 1.87 (1.39–2.51)
- **Adjusted aOR (95% CI)**: 1.81 (1.24–2.65)
- **p-value**: p < 0.001 (crude comparison)
- **Subgroup/notes**: Supplementary analysis (≥3 losses) showed similar risk. Authors note "caregiver bias" (elective CS in high-risk patients) as a possible explanation.

### Preterm Delivery (<37 Weeks)
- **RM group**: 16.2% (denominator n = 234 for the figure; exact n from subset of 1,522 available with data)
- **Control group**: 7.5% (figure shows 7.6% in one place; denominator is subset of 1,522)
- **Crude OR/RR (95% CI)**: 2.39 (1.55–3.67)
- **Adjusted aOR (95% CI)**: 2.52 (1.56–4.08)
- **p-value**: p < 0.001
- **Subgroup/notes**: Supplementary analysis with ≥3 losses showed similar risks. Gestational age at delivery excluded from the adjustment model for this outcome due to collinearity.

### Very Preterm Delivery (<32 Weeks)
- **RM group**: 4.6% (denominator = subset of 1,520 available)
- **Control group**: 0.6%
- **Crude OR/RR (95% CI)**: 7.92 (3.02–20.77)
- **Adjusted aOR (95% CI)**: 7.02 (2.41–20.46)
- **p-value**: p < 0.001
- **Subgroup/notes**: Remarkably large effect size; authors note this is consistent across both primary (≥2) and supplementary (≥3) definitions of RM

### Preeclampsia
- **RM group**: 3.2% (denominator = subset of 1,652 available)
- **Control group**: 1.2%
- **Crude OR/RR (95% CI)**: 2.74 (1.12–6.67)
- **Adjusted aOR (95% CI)**: 2.81 (0.95–8.27)
- **p-value**: p = 0.021 (crude); adjusted not significant (CI crosses 1.0)
- **Subgroup/notes**: Crude association significant; adjusted association loses significance (wide CI, small case numbers)

### Gestational Diabetes Mellitus (GDM)
- **RM group**: 24.2% (denominator = subset of 1,592 available)
- **Control group**: 26.1%
- **Crude OR/RR (95% CI)**: 0.90 (0.65–1.27)
- **Adjusted aOR (95% CI)**: 0.69 (0.47–1.02)
- **p-value**: p = 0.557 (crude)
- **Subgroup/notes**: No significant association with GDM. Note: high background prevalence of GDM in both groups (24–26%) in this UAE population.

### Blood Loss During Delivery
- **RM group**: Mean ± SD: 446 ± 306 mL
- **Control group**: Mean ± SD: 355 ± 276 mL
- **Crude OR/RR (95% CI)**: 1.36 (1.13–1.63) per SD
- **Adjusted aOR (95% CI)**: 1.03 (0.99–1.01) — note: appears to be a typographical issue in the paper as the CI of 0.99–1.01 does not encompass OR of 1.03; the adjusted result was described as non-significant
- **p-value**: p < 0.05 (crude); not significant after adjustment
- **Subgroup/notes**: Crude association with increased blood loss attenuated and lost significance after adjustment for caesarean section (CS). Authors attribute the attenuation to confounding by CS status (manual placental removal in CS associated with more blood loss).

## Group F: Quality Notes
- **Data source quality**: Moderate-high — prospective cohort design with medical record extraction and standardized data collection; representative of the Emirati pregnant population in Al Ain (three major health institutions). Response bias possible as this is a voluntary cohort. Relatively small RM group (n = 234).
- **Confounder adjustment**: Partial — adjusted for age, gravidity, body weight, and gestational age at delivery. Unable to adjust for ART in the current pregnancy (data confidential), etiology of RM, or uterine anomalies. Consanguinity collected but not included in regression models.
- **Outcome validation**: Hospital medical records; outcomes defined and extracted by data collectors; no ICD codes specifically mentioned
- **Bias acknowledged by authors**: Yes — acknowledged inability to adjust for ART in current pregnancy; inability to categorize RM by etiology; potential selection bias as this is a voluntary cohort; cannot capture a complete register of miscarriages (interim analysis). Residual confounding by factors not collected (thrombophilia, uterine anomalies, PCOS, thyroid disorders).
- **Other notes**: This is the only study in the collection from the Middle East (UAE) and the only study specifically reporting on an Emirati population. High prevalence of consanguinity (44–47%) and GDM (24–26%) in this population may make results not directly generalizable. The supplementary analysis (≥3 losses) showed similar results to the primary analysis (≥2 losses), supporting robustness across RM definitions. The study is the first population-based cohort to report RM prevalence and outcomes in the UAE.
